When you finally understand Republican survivorship bias, you finally understand why the party never collapses no matter how many of its own voters die.

Because Republicans aren’t the party of “the strong.”

They’re the party of whoever survived last year’s policies.

That’s the real calculus.

And nobody wants to say it out loud, but the numbers don’t lie:

Republicans are on more Medicaid. More SNAP. More disability. More Social Security. More rural subsidies. More federal assistance across the board.

And yet they vote for the politicians who keep gutting the very systems keeping them alive.

Why?

Because Republican policies don’t just hurt Democrats.

## They kill Republicans.

And the party has learned to treat that as a filter—a renewal cycle—a way to harden whatever remains.

---

But here’s the part that would cause a meltdown if anyone in D.C. ever said it:

Both parties know this.

Republicans know their policies shorten their voters’ lives. Democrats know Republicans know. And everyone stays quiet because once you say it plainly, the entire political theater falls apart.

Because then you’d have to admit the unholy truth:

## Every U.S. policy ever passed has only ever been one thing—a mortality rate adjustment.

Healthcare? **Mortality**.

Wages? **Mortality**.

Pollution? **Mortality**.

Gun policy? **Mortality**.

Abortion bans? **Mortality**.

Voting access? **Mortality**.

Medicaid expansion? **Mortality**.

Rural hospital closures? **Mortality**.

Climate response? **Mortality**.

Drug policy? **Mortality.**

> Everything comes down to this: Who lives longer, and who dies sooner. Who gets protected, and who gets exposed.

So when you vote for Republicans, and they pass Republican policy, you need to understand something nobody will tell you:

Your death is already factored in.

Your cancer cluster? Priced in.

Your uninsurable chronic illness? Priced in.

Your fentanyl-risk community? Priced in.

Your collapsing hospital? Priced in.

Your shortened lifespan? Priced in.

This isn’t a debate.

This is a mortality curve with a ballot box taped to it.

And Republican survivorship bias is the quiet engine behind the whole thing—the ideology that only the last ones standing deserve to be counted for one more election cycle.

So just to give you the clearest opinion on it:

Laws cause death. It how politics works.

When you vote for somebody you’re deciding who’s dying.
