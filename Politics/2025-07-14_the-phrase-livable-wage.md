**"The phrase** ***livable wage*** **is the most polite form of economic violence I've ever heard."**

What is a "livable" wage? Livable to *who?* For how long? With what dignity? And how many skipped meals are considered reasonable in that equation?

You know what *livable* means in this economy? It means:

You don't die fast enough to disrupt business.

It means:

You make just enough to *come back tomorrow.*

Not enough to **rest**. Not enough to **breathe**. Not enough to **leave**. Just enough to keep showing up *grateful.*

The phrase itself is gentrified. “Livable Wage” is a gentrified phrase.

*Livable wage* used to mean **I can raise my kid, eat good food, sleep in peace, and not fear the landlord.** Now it means **"I can afford gas station ramen and pray the toothache goes away on its own."**

When business owners say "We can't afford to pay a livable wage," what they're really saying is:

> "We need your poverty to keep our profit margin stable."
>
> "We need your exhaustion to stay in power."
>
> "We built a system that only works if your soul stays discounted."

They want you to live in the **gray zone between breakdown and submission.**

Where you don't *technically* starve, but you're never well-fed enough to leave.

Because a livable wage was never about living. It was about **controlling how much you have to give.**

The bare minimum you'll tolerate.

The slowest path to collapse.

The least amount of money capitalism can offer without sparking a riot.

And here's the real sting: The moment you ask for more, you're called entitled. Ungrateful. Unrealistic.

> They say: "You should be happy to have a job."
>
> But what they mean is: "You should be happy to be owned in installments."

Because the system isn't broken. It's just working *exactly as designed:* You're supposed to confuse exhaustion with opportunity.

You don't want a *livable* wage.

You want a *worthy* one.

A **dignified** one. One that lets your soul stretch in the morning instead of calculating survival by lunch.

So stop asking for livable.

Ask for **restorative. Sustainable. Expansive. Soft. Unapologetic. Beautiful.**

Because you were not born to budget your breath.

---

So **what do we** ***actually*** **want?** Because *"livable wage"* was never the true demand. It was the **settled-for language of survival** framed as progress.

### "Livable Wage" is a Floor

But humans weren't meant to *live* at the floor. We were meant to live in:

### **A Soul-Compatible Economy**

Not just survival—but structure that doesn't require self-betrayal to function inside.

**Real ask:** *"I want to be sustained in a way that doesn't hollow me out."*

No job should demand your **identity fracture** to keep your healthcare. No paycheck should require **disconnecting from your child's milestones** or **spiritual center** to maintain.

### **Narrative Dignity**

Not just enough money—but the **right to be unmonitored, unpoliced, unperformed for capital.**

**Real ask:** *"I want to matter even when I'm not producing."*

Because "livable" still implies that **value is earned through work.** But the true need is:

**To be valued** ***without needing to justify your breath.***

### **Economic Softness**

Not comfort for the rich, **but systemic gentleness.**

**Real ask:** *"I want to make mistakes without it becoming a generational curse."*

You don't want just money. You want **room.** To fall, recover, try again—**without ruin.**

### **Communal Infrastructure**

Not just personal income, but **shared systems that hold us.**

**Real ask:** *"I want to belong to something that sustains us all—so it's not me vs. rent."*

We were never meant to individually out-hustle the collapse. We're craving a world where **our wellbeing isn't a competition.**

### **Rest-Backed Freedom**

The true economy is *time, not money.*

**Real ask:** *"I want enough freedom that my nervous system can stop bracing."*

Because what's the point of "livable" if your soul is still surviving every clock-in?

We don't want livable wages. We want:

- Soul-compatible labor
- Time sovereignty
- Narrative dignity
- Gentle consequences
- Communal rootedness
- Safety without surveillance
- And a future that doesn't require us to fracture in order to earn light.

This isn't a demand. It's a **reminder of what we were never meant to settle for.**
