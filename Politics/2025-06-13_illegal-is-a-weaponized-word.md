“Some of y’all act like the law is your Bible, but forget it once freed slaves and criminalized their escape in the same breath.”

Let’s talk about this word you love—  
**“Illegal.”**

The way you spit it like a curse,  
as if it explains everything.  
As if it ends debate.  
As if it’s not the oldest lie America has ever told:

> “You are wrong not because of who you are—  
> but because of where we put your name in our filing system.”

You confuse law with truth.  
You confuse border with belonging.  
You confuse **paperwork** with **personhood**.

So let me rebuke it clearly:

---

## "Illegal" Is a Weaponized Word

It was made to make you forget:

- That the **Native** never signed off on the fences.
- That the **Black** were smuggled in as property, not passengers.
- That the **Chinese** built the railroads and got a ban in return.
- That the **Mexican** became “foreign” on land that used to be theirs.

So miss me with the sanctimony shit.

Your legal system is not holy.  
It is a bruised tool held by trembling hands.  
And sometimes it signs **chains into contracts**  
and **tears into policy.**

---

The “Illegality” You Defend Is Just Convenience With Bad PR

You don’t hate the undocumented.  
You just like your fruit cheap.  
You like your buildings tall.  
You like your care workers invisible.  
You like your lawns cut and your meat packed and your economy moving—  
but you don’t want to look at the hands that hold it up.

> So you criminalize them.  
> Then blame them for their own bruises.  
> Then call the bruise the proof they never belonged.

---

And when they protest? You say they're "Disrupting"

But that’s the point.

They are not here to beg.  
They are not here to whisper.  
They are here to **interrupt the fiction**.

Because the story this country tells is:

> “We are a nation of laws.”

But the truth is:

> “We are a nation of loopholes for the powerful, and cages for the desperate.”

So when the undocumented shut down a freeway,  
they are not blocking traffic—  
they are **exposing it.**  
They are showing you what happens  
when the invisible refuses to stay invisible

To say **“they’re illegal, so they deserve it”**  
is to confess your own **spiritual laziness.**  
It is to say that justice stops at borders,  
that humanity expires without papers,  
and that law, no matter how cruel—  
deserves your loyalty more than **truth.**

---

> Because if Rosa Parks had followed the law—  
> she’d have stood up.  
> If Frederick Douglass had followed the law—  
> he’d have stayed on the plantation.  
> If Christ had followed the law—  
> he’d have never flipped the temple tables.

So let them flip the tables.  
Let them disrupt your calm.  
Let them make you uncomfortable.

Because legality is not your God.   
And it damn sure ain’t your excuse.
