## “The shutdown is over.”

**They just dragged the body off the street so the Christmas lights wouldn’t look awkward.**

If this was over, they wouldn’t have stamped an expiration date on the side of the country like milk.

January 30.  
That’s not a budget.  
That’s a **timer**.

We’re living inside the bomb shelter **while they argue over who gets to twist the detonator.**

---

## They said *reopening protects Americans*.

But 40 days of missed income is a lifetime for a poor household.

They said *it was necessary*. But the damage didn’t “pause” **it metastasized.**

They said *workers will get backpay.*

But contractors go hungry.

Caretakers go broke.

Children lose stability that a check in January can’t resurrect.

They said *benefits continue*.

But SNAP was throttled into a humanitarian crisis, **mothers choosing between food and heat** while politicians debated photo ops.

They said *credit damage can be fixed.*

But your rent had a longer conversation with your landlord than Congress had with each other.

Your credit card didn’t care about “furlough.”

**It cared about the minimum payment.**

They said *this is behind us*.

But January is in front of us like a cliff pretending to be a calendar.

---

**If the government really reopened, why does every household still look like the shutdown never left?**

- Why is January already preloading the same pain?
- Why was the fuse set to 50 days instead of a fiscal year?
- Why is every agency whispering “freeze everything until the next collapse”?
- Why do workers still feel like they’re budgeting around an earthquake?

Because it **wasn’t a reopening.**

It was an **intermission.**

The crisis is on a lunch break.

---

### Picture this:

A house catches fire.

The fire department hoses it for ten minutes, just enough to keep it from collapsing before Christmas pictures.

They leave.

The smoke keeps rising.

The beams keep cracking.

The fire keeps breathing in the walls.

And taped to the front door?

A note:

**“We’ll be back January 30.”**

They didn’t put the fire out.

They scheduled the next one.

---

# **THE DAMAGE: What the 40 days actually cost**

Let’s speak it without euphemism:

**Forty days is long enough to ruin someone.**

- Credit scores fell through trap doors.
- Savings evaporated.
- Rent got “re-negotiated” with a threat standing behind it.
- SNAP families were pushed into emergency mode.
- Mental health cracked.
- Contractors lost two months of income they’ll *never* get back.
- Federal workers lived in suspended animation.

And now the country is being told: “Relax. It’s handled.”

**Handled where?  
Handled how?  
Handled by who?**

January is not a return to normal.

January is the same fist you just escaped, coming back with brass knuckles.

---

**If they could pin you down for 40 days once, what makes you think January isn’t the rematch they’ve been preparing for?**

You survived the shutdown.

But the shutdown hasn’t stopped.

It just stepped out of the room to let the bomb keep ticking.
