Everyday I see, **“We’re not angry enough,”** so I’m going to tell you the truth.

# **The system can withstand anger indefinitely.**

## Indefinitely.

It doesn’t flinch. It doesn’t panic. It doesn’t even listen.

Anger is readable. Anger is predictable. Anger is one of its renewable resources.

You think rage scares power? Rage is how power knows you’re still inside the cage.

And let’s stop being polite.

#### **White people—the government does not fear your rebellion either.**

Not even a little.

Your guns don’t scare it. Your flags don’t scare it. Your cosplay revolution doesn’t scare it.

They already ran the numbers. They already know you can’t outlast supply chains, you can’t secede from insulin, you can’t shoot your way into semiconductors, you can’t militia your way into legitimacy.

You can hold land. You cannot hold **civilization**.

Now sit with this—because this is where the floor drops out.

**The national horror fantasy.**

White nationalist groups seize land in the South. Guns. Militias. Checkpoints. “Freedom.”

This is the image people use to scare themselves.

Here’s what actually happens.

The system doesn’t *hope* fragmentation comes. It **counts on it**.

Legitimacy doesn’t erode, it **detonates** on contact.

Insurance doesn’t pull out. It **disappears overnight**.

Trade locks its doors.

Fuel doesn’t run low. It **never arrives**.

Medicine doesn’t become scarce. It ceases to exist.

Dialysis stops.

Insulin stops.

Antibiotics stops.

Banks freeze because risk models trigger.

Hospitals shut down because liability evaporates.

Food rots because shipping routes don’t recognize fantasies.

No tanks. No speeches. No dramatic crackdown.

Just **silence**—and the sound of people realizing too late that civilization is not held together by dominance, but by a thousand invisible agreements they never learned how to replace.

The system doesn’t crush this scenario.

It lets it **bleed itself dry** until the word “freedom” tastes like ash and the people left standing are begging for the very continuity they thought they could conquer.

That’s the grief underneath the fantasy.

Not that domination failed—that **dependency was exposed**.

And Freedom of speech?

Let’s kill the romance right now.

Speech is free because **speech doesn’t move power**.

You can scream forever. You can articulate perfectly. You can go viral.

The system will index you, monetize you, and wait for you to burn out.

Speech keeps you *inside*—arguing with a structure that doesn’t need to answer.

Now the part people still refuse to believe.

## **The right to bear arms staying this long is proof of one thing.**

Not freedom. Not trust.

**Confidence.**

The state didn’t hesitate to disarm you. It **decided it didn’t need to**.

If civilian weapons posed a real threat to power, they’d be gone.

No debate.

No mythology.

No culture war.

Gone.

The fact that they’re still here means the calculus already closed.

They already know this:

You can’t aim at credit. You can’t shoot logistics. You can’t intimidate insurance. You can’t threaten supply chains. You can’t hold a gun to electricity and make it comply.

You don’t fight the state and get crushed.

You fight the state and **nothing happens**—until your prescriptions stop refilling, your cards stop clearing, your trucks stop arriving, your hospitals stop accepting liability, and your people start turning on each other over scarcity.

No sirens. No show of force. No martial law fantasy.

Just systems doing what they were designed to do when risk exceeds tolerance.

Weapons don’t scare power because power no longer lives where violence can reach it.

And the state doesn’t delay.

It **lets reality do the killing** while it keeps its hands clean.

That’s why the right persists.

Because it’s not dangerous.

It’s **irrelevant at scale**.

And irrelevance is safer than suppression.

Now let’s go even deeper to the lie people don’t realize they’re still clinging to.

**Corporate accountability.**

**Legal wins.**

**Court decisions.**

Listen carefully.

If a victory must be *recognized by the system* to exist, it is not liberation.

**It’s a permission slip.**

Corporate rights don’t exist because corporations are “evil.” They exist because corporations are **perfect system citizens**.

They don’t age. They don’t feel. They don’t mourn. They don’t hesitate.

They obey procedure, scale infinitely, and absorb consequence as cost.

The law doesn’t restrain power. The law **formats** it.

So when you win in court, when a regulation passes, when a corporation gets fined, that’s not a rupture.

That’s the system saying: **“We can survive this too.”**

The nightmare scenario isn’t authoritarian takeover. It isn’t militias. It isn’t collapse.

The real horror is **continuity**.

A system that can absorb rage, rebellion, reform, exposure, and scandal without ever needing to change what it *is*.

That’s why anger feels righteous but hollow. That’s why victories feel real but thin. That’s why something in your chest still aches afterward.

Because deep down you know—nothing fundamental moved.

This is the grief nobody names.

You weren’t fighting the system. You were proving it could survive you with your own rage.

And that realization hurts more than oppression ever did.

## Because it means the solution isn’t escalation.

It’s **withdrawal of belief**.

Not violence. Not chaos. Not performance.

But people quietly refusing to let their lives be the fuel.

Refusing to treat legitimacy as external. Refusing to confuse recognition with worth. Refusing to organize their being around institutions that only know how to manage bodies and reactions.

And here’s the part you take with you.

Do not ask me what to do.

When you encounter the world, **do not let rage drown your clarity**.

Figure out the will to execute this one command:

**I will not let this govern me.**

Not today. Not in my body. Not in my relationships. Not in my sense of self.

This isn’t retreat. It’s reclamation.

This is the snap. The bone going back where it always belonged.

A painful kind of hope—not the kind that promises comfort, but the kind that returns your life to you.

And once enough people live from that place, not loudly, not violently, but coherently, the system doesn’t fall in flames.

It starves.

And starvation is the one thing it was never designed to survive.
