**“We never abolished slavery — we rewrote it into a budget.”**

“You ever wonder how we got from plantations… to prison labor? How slavery got *legalized* again without ever needing a whip?”

> *"Neither slavery nor involuntary servitude... shall exist, except as a punishment for crime."*

And there it is.

A constitutional back door.  
Slavery 2.0 — branded as justice, sold as order, funded as labor.

We call them “inmates.”  
But **they’re making products for Walmart, cleaning supplies for Amazon, working slaughterhouses for McDonald's** — often for **pennies on the hour**, or nothing at all.

No healthcare.  
No bargaining power.  
No choice.

This isn’t “prison labor.”  
This is **supply-chain slavery**.  
And it’s legal.

---

White society once received its freedom *with conditions*.  
A **velvet collar** of comfort, credit, and curated opportunity.  
Black people were offered no such collar — just chains that changed shape.

Now?  
A new collar is being built — made of biometric surveillance, invisible fences, wage compression, digital tracking, and data-extracted existence.

And we’re all in line.

---

But **Black and Brown people** were *always* the test subjects.  
From convict leasing to chain gangs, to prison telecom extortion.  
Now? They call it “rehabilitation.”  
But the **only thing getting rehabilitated is the profit margin.**

---

- **Amazon. Coca-Cola. McDonald’s.**
- **Microsoft. Kroger. Walmart. Home Depot.**
- **Tyson Foods. General Mills. AT&T. FedEx.**
- **Victoria’s Secret. Boeing. Lockheed Martin. Chevron.**

Some use it directly.  
Some just wash their hands through suppliers.

**“If you eat it, ship it, or wear it — chances are, someone in prison made part of it.”**

---

This is **legalized labor theft**  
wrapped in punishment,  
disguised as justice,  
and sold to the public as safety.

We’re not just talking about a prison system.  
We’re talking about a **nationwide slave engine** running behind your everyday purchases.

---

When you ask why Black communities resist “unity,” or “trusting the system”?  
It’s because they remember.  
Because **our bodies never stopped being currency.**  
And because every policy they call “reform”…  
was just **a new plantation with better branding**.

---

> **“Slavery didn’t end — it evolved into logistics. And the 13th Amendment is the barcode.”**
