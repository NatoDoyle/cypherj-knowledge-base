To understand the Sudan and the other conflicts that are taking place in Africa.

There’s a pipeline.

Mines in conflict zones produce metal. Armed actors tax it. It leaves in crates that say ‘equipment.’ It lands in cities that melt and re-stamp it. Paper makes the bars clean. Money buys guns. Repeat.

> **Mines → Tax → Crate → Melt → Paper → Cash → Guns**

## **To understand how they get the gold to market:**

Extraction in Africa → First wash in Dubai/UAE → Refining & certification in Switzerland → Global markets, banks, ETFs, vaults. That loop is the shadow standard: gold becomes anonymous collateral that backs off-ledger deals and weapons buys.

> **Africa → Dubai → Switzerland → Global Finance**

## **Example A (ground side: Sudan / RSF)**

On the ground in Sudan: miners dig, militias collect, brokers move crates to transit hubs, planes fly to Gulf refineries. Reports show hundreds of tonnes and billions in value flowing into UAE channels since 2023.

That cash funds procurement and pays fighters.

## **Example B (the buyer side: oligarch / sanctioned actor)**

The buyer: a private trust buys ‘LBMA-stamped’ bars from a Swiss desk. Those bars once passed through the Gulf. The trust uses the bars as collateral or swaps them into currency. Liquidity appears. No bank has to ask where the ore was boxed.”  
On-screen text: **Swiss stamp hides origin → Bars → Collateral → Liquidity**

**Who’s been hit / who’s next**

Where this has already hit:

- Ghana.

Active now:

**How the shadow standard props up big actors**

Why it matters to big finance:

Once the bar is certified, banks and ETFs treat it as clean collateral.

Corporations, bullion banks and central banks use gold as a shadow reserve, buying, leasing, lending on it. In crises, gold flows become private liquidity while public debt keeps growing.”

> **Certified bars = collateral → Banks & corporations use it → Shadow reserve**

**What would break the machine (advocacy asks)**

If we want to injure the RSF and shut the pipeline, demand real, targeted action not slogans.

Three things:

1) Public feedstock audits for refineries (UAE & Switzerland)

2) Beneficial-ownership transparency in free zones

3) Safe, licensed buying centers in mining zones plus humanitarian cash for communities.”

Say Free Sudan, and also ask:

Who took the gold? Demand the ledger. Make spectacle meet accountability. Because until the paper is honest, the killing will be financed in plain sight.
