A lot of y’all say we’re *losing* freedom of speech.

But I’m just wondering when have you ever seen it.

Because free speech was never contained in the Constitution it was just wished upon there.

Real free speech has never been an enforced right in this country’s history.

It was a possibility.  
A flare in the dark every time someone dared to walk against silence.

Let me make it super clear.

## Black people have *never* had free speech.

From the plantations to the prisons, every word a black person has ever spoken has cost blood.

- A hymn could get you whipped.
- A pamphlet could get you lynched.
- A truth could get you assassinated, and you’d be dead and it’d still be true

And white folks?

You thought you had it but even you only kept it until it came down to women, until it came down to workers, until it came down to anyone who threatened the empire you inherited.

Then you learned quick: **your speech was free only inside the lines they drew.**

---

So don’t tell me about “losing” it.

## The truth is America never let it live.

They trained you to worship a Constitution a veil that lets money dictate what God gave you freely.

They told you: ***look, you can say what you want*****—**while corporations decide what’s visible, while algorithms decide what’s profitable, while culture trains you to cancel yourself before you even open your mouth.

---

And so what if the government can’t jail you?

You already exist in a prison in your own mind.

You only speak to each other when the surveillance network drops its pen.

You think you’re free because you can whisper, but you’re already gagged.

---

**And hate speech?**  
Here’s a controversial take on that:

If speech were truly free hate speech would never be a problem.

Because hate only survives when greater society bypasses grief.

- When wounds go unspoken, hate offers a scapegoat.
- When history is buried, hate hands you a target.
- When sorrow has no witness, hate performs as noise.

Grief metabolized becomes solidarity.  
Grief bypassed becomes blame.

**Grief metabolized becomes solidarity.  
Grief bypassed becomes blame.**

So give grief its voice, and hate has nothing left to feed on.

It withers—not because it’s banned, but because it can’t scale in a room already filled with truth.

And hear me well:

## The only thing God has ever given you is BREATH.

That is the freedom.

That is the speech.

Not the parchment. Not the platform. Not permission.

Freedom of speech is not what you inherit.

It’s the flame you keep alive.

> Every time you speak past fear. That’s free speech.   
> Every time you refuse the script. That’s free speech.  
> Every time you let your lungs remind you—your voice was never theirs to give.

---

Because it’s not about protecting a right.

It’s about *living a possibility.*
