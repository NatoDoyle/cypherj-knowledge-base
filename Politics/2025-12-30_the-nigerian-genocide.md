Everyone keeps asking what’s happening in Nigeria.

**They’re asking the wrong question.**

The real question is: “**why** ***this*** **story, and why** ***now*****.”**

Because Nigeria didn’t suddenly become violent.

And Sudan didn’t suddenly collapse.

What changed was **which narrative was allowed to touch the money.**

Watch the swap.

### In Sudan, they said *humanitarian catastrophe*.

Famine. Displacement. Chaos.

That language shows up **after** the ledger is already exposed.

After the gold is traced.

After the war economy is too obvious to deny.

### In Nigeria, they say *Christian genocide*.

Moral. Emotional. Absolute.

A story that demands outrage —but **never asks who’s paid.**

That’s not coincidence.

That’s containment.

Because the moment you talk economics:

- You have to talk about trade hubs.
- You have to talk about refineries.
- You have to talk about who buys what, where it’s melted, and which allies would suddenly be in the frame.

So instead, they give you faith.

Faith lets them fly surveillance.

Faith lets them threaten intervention.

Faith lets them posture without auditing a single shipment.

Faith **blocks enforcement**.

Sudan crossed the line where the money couldn’t hide anymore.

Nigeria hasn’t — not yet.

So Nigeria gets a narrative that keeps the books closed.

- Same region.
- Same rising gold output.
- Same instability pressure.
- Different mask.

### This is [[Narrative Warfare|narrative warfare]].

When a country is still valuable, you moralize it.

When it’s already collapsing, you humanitarianize it.

Both keep you from saying the same forbidden sentence:

**“Follow the metal.”**

Because if you follow the metal, you don’t just find militias.

You find clearinghouses.

You find paperwork.

You find respectable institutions whose entire job is to make violence tradable.

So no — this isn’t about whether Christians are dying.

People are dying everywhere this machine runs.

This is about **which story stops you from asking who profits**.

Nigeria is being held in narrative suspension.

Sudan already fell through.

And the warning isn’t that Nigeria will become Sudan.

The warning is this:

**The moment Nigeria becomes economically radioactive, the story will change overnight — and everyone will pretend they were surprised.**
