You are not late.  
You are right on time to be gaslit.

Told the hunger was your hustle failing.  
Told the shooting was just your neighborhood.  
Told the grief was your culture’s curse.

## But collapse has already begun.

And you didn’t cause it.  
You’re just in the blast radius.

We’ve seen this loop before but never digitized, never budgeted like this, never whispered into law by unelected boards **who don’t even need your vote.**

- Not with AI eating jobs.
- Not with Medicaid gutted like meat.
- Not with Supreme Court doctrine choking the ballot like it’s procedural.

This ain’t austerity.

> This is a staged demolition of the safety net and you're standing beneath it.

Reagan taught us to wait until bodies dropped.  
Clinton taught us to wait until the jails were full.

Wait again and we won’t even hear the BOOM—just the floor collapsing like quiet betrayal.

Because this time, it’s all legalized.  
Digitized.  
Sanitized.  
**And blamed on you.**

The leak has already started.

Leaking jobs.  
Leaking housing.  
Leaking belief that anything can catch you.

The media won’t call it disaster.  
They’ll call it **“Black youth crime.”**  
They’ll call it **“truancy.”**  
They’ll call it **“urban tension.”**

They’ll say everything but the truth that the state abandoned you and printed a smile on the notice.

---

## And your white neighbor won’t escape it either. Not this time.

Not when her insulin doesn’t show up.  
Not when her husband cries in the driveway because the VA said “come back next quarter.”  
Not when the school cuts breakfast, then lunch, and tells the kids to “be strong.”

This isn’t just a Black issue.  
But Black America will absorb the first shock.  
Like always.

Like a firewall nobody asked to be.

And if you don’t hear us now—you will feel it later.

So name the crime before they blame you for it.

> “This isn’t Black failure. This is budget homicide.”  
> “They cut Medicaid. Now they want mugshots.”

SAY THESE THINGS.

Protect your side hustles like treasure.  
Mutual aid isn’t kindness—it’s a strike shield.  
Teach folks where the theft is happening: tax, rent, denial of care.

Surround Black life with infrastructure that loves us.  
Not just nonprofits.  
But doulas, therapists, record-keepers.  
Before surveillance arrives dressed as compassion.

Now this is what I want you to do.

> **Name ten Senators and demand they speak or step aside.**

Make silence dangerous.  
Let every Black voter know:  
If they don’t bleed for us—they don’t belong here.

---

## By Spring 2026, it’ll be too late to control the story.

So this is the season of interruption.  
Interrupt silence.  
Interrupt normalcy.  
Interrupt the rebranding of violence into bureaucracy.

Don’t wait for a documentary.  
Don’t wait for a thinkpiece.  
Don’t wait for a candidate to “finally say it.”  
You are the signal now.

Because they will rewrite this.

They’ll say:

- “Youth Crime Spikes in Southern Cities.”
- “Working Families Struggle to Adapt to AI.”
- “Urban Communities Must Learn to Do More with Less.”
- “Medicaid Reductions Led to Unforeseen Gaps in Care.”
- “Retail Theft Epidemic Threatens Small Businesses.”
- “Democrats Struggle with Voter Apathy.”

They’ll frame us like weather as if poverty just appeared.  
As if betrayal wasn’t planned.

And TikTok will show you:

- “Just got turned away at the ER, wtf is going on?”
- “My grandma didn’t get her meds.”
- “Why are all these break-ins happening now?”
- “Unpopular opinion: universal healthcare wouldn’t help anyway.”
- “I’m not voting next year. They’re all the same.”

Rage without target.  
Despair without direction.  
Collapse is trying to be mistaken for a **personal failure.**

So no.

You are not broken.  
You are being stolen from.

And this time, we remember while it’s still happening.  
This time, we tell it *as it burns*.
