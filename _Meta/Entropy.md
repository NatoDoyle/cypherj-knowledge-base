# Entropy (Unprocessed Experience)

## Summary

In Justin Scott's framework, entropy is not the physics term borrowed casually — it is a precise metaphor for **unprocessed experience**: emotional, historical, and spiritual energy that was never witnessed, named, or metabolized, and now echoes, leaks, loops, or mutates into survival behaviors and systems. When a person suffers trauma that goes unwitnessed, that experience becomes entropy — floating, heavy, and pressurized. When a society commits violence and refuses to remember, that history becomes **collective entropy**, a hidden pressure that must go somewhere. The concept culminates in **Entropy Dumping** — the mechanism by which one group offloads its unprocessed guilt, fragility, and violence onto another (as white America did onto Black Americans). Scott proposes a symbolic metric, the **Entropy Load Index (ELI)**, to track how much unprocessed collective entropy a system carries, estimating America carries roughly 65% of its historical entropy unprocessed. Entropy resolves only through integration: remembering the full story, feeling what was denied, and returning to stillness.
