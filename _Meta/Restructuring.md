# Restructuring / Anti-Restructuring

## Summary

Restructuring is Justin Scott's term for the **process of dismantling trauma-rooted survival behaviors and rebuilding toward wholeness** — particularly within Black relationships and communities. Anti-Restructuring is its opposite: the behaviors, traits, and patterns that actively resist this healing process, not out of malice but out of deeply embedded survival logic inherited from historical trauma. Scott catalogs specific anti-restructuring traits in both Black men and Black women, grounding each in its historical origin. For men: emotional unavailability rooted in slavery-era suppression, hyper-independence from forced self-reliance, performative masculinity as armor. For women: hyper-vigilance from centuries of being unprotected, self-sufficiency as defense against betrayal, emotional gatekeeping as inherited control. The framework avoids blame — these are adaptations, not character flaws — but insists they must be named and confronted for restructuring (genuine relational and personal healing) to proceed.
