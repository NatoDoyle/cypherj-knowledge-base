# Parallel Systems

## Summary

The Parallel Systems are Justin Scott's names for the **survival architectures** that emerge when Proto-Fears go unmetabolized. They are not pathologies to be destroyed but distortions to be understood — each one a mutation of an original wound into a coping mechanism that eventually replaces identity itself. The nine named systems are: **Maw** (false image construction — "If you eat this, you'll be like God"), **Keep** (hiding and withdrawal — "Hide yourself"), **Flame** (blame and projection — "Blame her. Blame him"), **Thorn** (suffering as identity — "We must suffer now"), **Cog** (control through labor — "Control the world through toil"), **Reverb** (shame loops — "Remember your shame. Never forget"), **Prism** (mask-wearing — "Cover yourself. They must not see you"), **Pulse** (compulsive hunger — "You are now flesh. And flesh is hunger"), and **Hearth** (presence without distortion — the memory of the garden, the integrated state). Scott frames these through Genesis: before the Fall, Adam and Eve existed in Hearth energy — naked, unashamed, present. The Fall birthed the other eight systems. The journey is not forward but inward: the Hearth was always there, buried under entropy.
