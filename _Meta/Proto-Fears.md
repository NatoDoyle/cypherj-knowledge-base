# Proto-Fears

## Summary

The Proto-Fears are Justin Scott's term for **eight primordial wounds** — raw, pre-mythic fractures of being that exist before culture, theology, or personality shape them. They are not evil; they are truths too heavy to carry. When left unmetabolized, each Proto-Fear mutates into one or more **Parallel Systems** — survival architectures that distort identity and connection. The eight Proto-Fears are: the Fear of Time ("I am running out"), the Fear of Separation ("I am alone"), the Fear of Meaninglessness ("None of this matters"), the Fear of Chaos ("It's all out of control"), the Fear of Being Unseen ("No one truly knows me"), the Fear of Insufficiency ("I am not enough"), the Fear of Pain ("It will hurt again"), and the Fear of Loss of Identity ("Who am I without this?"). Each has a corresponding **Hearth Response** — the integrated truth that dissolves the wound. The Hearth is not something created but remembered: "the garden we carry," buried under entropy. Scott roots this framework in a theological reading of Genesis: before the Fall there was presence without distortion; the Fall birthed the systems.
