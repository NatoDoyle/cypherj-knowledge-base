# Light-Identity Unified Theory (LIUT)

## Summary

The Light-Identity Unified Theory is Justin Scott's most ambitious framework — a metaphysical hypothesis proposing that **consciousness is light focused through a metabolic lens**. In this model, identity is not a fixed possession but a temporary focusing of infinite awareness through a biological body. Death does not end consciousness but returns awareness to its unfocused, infinite state. Scott presents LIUT not as religious doctrine but as a testable hypothesis that bridges physics, theology, and psychology. The theory reframes entropy as a dimensional transfer function and positions the Parallel Systems as distortions of light — ways that focused awareness gets bent by trauma. The companion notes emphasize that LIUT is meant to be falsifiable and debatable, not worshipped. At its longest articulation (~14,000 words in "The Lie of Consensus Reality"), the theory extends into epistemology: consensus reality is "coordinated rendering" mistaken for objective truth, and only awareness and patterns truly exist.
