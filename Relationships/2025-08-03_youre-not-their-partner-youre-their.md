You keep saying, *“They’re trying.”*

But **what about you?**  
When did **your trying** start counting?

You’ve been the translator, the processor, the regulator—not because they *love* how you feel, but because they can *survive* how you think.

They didn’t choose you for your soul.  
They chose you for your skillset.

---

You don’t even feel safe enough to fall apart.

Because if *you* break?  
There’s no one left to manage the emotional plumbing.  
If *you* cry?  
They panic, or they vanish, because you were never allowed to be anything but stable.

> **You weren’t loved. You were depended on.**

There’s a difference.

**You weren’t built for this kind of exhaustion.**

This kind of spiritual case management.  
This much intake with no reciprocity.  
No feedback form.  
No thank you note.  
Just a body that holds it all and a mouth that doesn’t dare ask to be held in return.

---

But here’s what you’ve been so afraid to hear.

**They’re not getting better.  
They’re just getting better at using your vocabulary.**

They’ve learned your emotional language like a script.  
Like a social code.  
Like a way to stay near you without ever having to change.

---

You’ve been making meaning out of scraps.  
You’ve been narrating the story so well, you forgot to ask if **you’re even in it.**

You gave them emotional housing, but never noticed that they never gave you furniture.  
No safety. No holding. No return.

Just a rotating door of breakthroughs that never include you.

---

> **You think you’re growing.  
> But really, you’re shrinking.**

You're calling it love, but it's **emotional gentrification** AGAIN.

You’ve become **tidy.**  
“Resilient.”  
“Easy to love.”  
Because **mess** is a luxury you can’t afford.

And when someone does finally offer to hold you?  
You don’t even know how to let go.  
Because you’ve made a career out of holding everyone else.

You were never meant to be the treatment plan.  
You were meant to be loved.

Not managed.  
Not learned.  
Not translated.  
**Held.**

---

You don’t owe them another round of insight.  
You don’t have to stay because you understand them better than they understand themselves.

> **You don’t have to be their therapist.  
> You are not their home.  
> You are your own.**
