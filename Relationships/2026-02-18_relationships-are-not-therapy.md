Relationships are not therapy.

And the fact that we have to keep saying that means people are walking into love with a wound in one hand and a contract in the other.

You are not entering someone’s life to repair your childhood. You are not dating to regulate your nervous system. You are not there to be fixed. You are not there to fix.

And no one—no one—is here to heal your relationship with the world.

Not your partner. Not your spouse. Not the person who loves you.

If the world feels unsafe, if people feel untrustworthy, if life feels like rejection, romance is not a shortcut out of that.

---

## A relationship is not a repair shop. It’s a meeting point.

And most of us? We don’t meet people. We move into them.

We anticipate them. We manage them. We pre-soothe them. We “understand” them before they’ve even spoken.

We call it empathy. We call it maturity. We call it emotional intelligence.

But really?

We’re relating to an abstraction.

You built a version of them in your head, interacted with that all day, and then when the real person shows up, you react like they’re the ghost.

Because they just disrupted all your invisible labor.

### And then we misuse vulnerability.

Trust does not create vulnerability.

Vulnerability creates data. Data creates trust.

If you’re waiting until it’s safe, it’s not vulnerability. It’s staged exposure.

Vulnerability has risk baked in.

You might be misunderstood. You might be rejected. You might not be held.

And that’s the point.

Saying, “I can only be vulnerable if you can hold me” is like saying, “I can only jump if gravity promises not to work.”

That’s not courage. That’s negotiation.

Real vulnerability says: “I trust myself to survive the outcome.”

Not: “I require you to regulate it.”

### And “holding”?

Holding is not fixing. Holding is not agreeing. Holding is not absorbing someone’s emotions into your body. Holding is not soft compliance.

Holding is stability under exposure.

You say something heavy. I do not collapse. I do not panic. I do not make it about me. I stay.

That’s holding.

And there’s an irritation about all of this:

That people demand to be held before they’ve risked anything.

They want emotional insurance before they offer exposure.

They want someone to soothe their distrust of the world.

But your partner is not the ambassador of existence.

They are not here to correct your relationship with humanity.

They are here to meet you.

Love is not a clinic. It’s a crossing.

Two intact nervous systems. Standing in their own stability. Choosing contact.

Not to be repaired. Not to be regulated. Not to be rescued from the world.

But to be met.
