Sexualization is capitalism.

That’s not a metaphor.

That’s just what happens when attraction gets pushed through **market logic.**

Market logic has three rules:

> Be visible. Be legible. Be comparable.

Once those three rules enter desire, bodies stop being bodies.

They become **signals.**

- Hair signals.
- Makeup signals.
- Body signals.
- Clothing signals.
- Gym signals.
- Aesthetic signals.

People think they’re looking at attraction.

They’re not.

They’re reading **desirability packaging.**

And sexualization is not when someone dresses freely, confidently, or shows their body.

Sexualization happens when a **person gets reduced to a signal meant to trigger attention.**

---

## Sexualization trains the brain to look at people the way a grocery scanner reads products.

Quick signal. Instant judgment. Move on.

But erotic attraction doesn’t work like that.

> Erotic attraction runs on **mystery.**

And sexualization destroys mystery instantly.

Because sexualization replaces encounter with **maximum visibility.**

Everything is already announced. Everything is already exaggerated. Everything is already optimized to be understood in three seconds.

But fascination does not grow where everything is immediately understood.

Fascination grows in the space between noticing someone… and understanding them.

Sexualization collapses that space.

Which is why hyper-sexual environments often feel strangely **dead.**

Everything is visible. Nothing is intriguing. Everything is loud. Nothing unfolds.

Now here’s the uncomfortable part.

**Men drift toward sexualization in flirting because it’s the only language many of them were handed.**

Most men’s flirting language is just a combination of three categories:

- **Porn language.**
- **Locker-room language.**
- **Advertising language.**

And then they try to make intimacy out of that.

Think about it.

Porn language turns bodies into explicit stimulus.

Locker-room language turns bodies into competitive bragging.

Advertising language turns bodies into desirability slogans.

Put those three together and what do you get?

*“You’re hot.”*

*“Look at that body.”*

*“Damn.”*

That is not flirting.

That is a **product review.**

It isn’t even real desire.

It’s risk management.

Sexualized language signals intent quickly while protecting the speaker from vulnerability.

If she doesn’t respond, the retreat is already built in:

“I was just joking.”

But here’s the other side people rarely talk about.

---

## Women often dislike sexualization.

I have no clue why this isn’t obvious.

But modern environments quietly force participation in it.

Because sexualization is the fastest way to become **legible to attention systems.**

Attention systems reward spectacle. Spectacle rewards signals. Signals reward sexualization.

Which means people slowly get trained to convert their bodies into **advertising surfaces.**

And the strategy seems logical.

If visibility increases, attention increases.

But attention is not attraction.

Attention is **traffic.**

Attraction is singular.

And singular attraction requires something the market cannot tolerate.

**Mystery.**

Mystery requires interior life.

Mystery requires incomplete knowledge.

Mystery requires the possibility that someone is more than what can be scanned in three seconds.

And this is where the trap shows up inside relationships.

This is also why a relationship can be freaky all day and still die.

Because sexualization increases the stimulus while killing the mystery eroticism needs to breathe.

Sexualization can create intensity. I’m not dissuading that effect.

But intensity is not the same thing as erotic life.

Intensity burns.

Eroticism **breathes.**

---

#### That’s why real flirting sounds completely different from sexualized language.

Real flirting sounds like attention.

It sounds like:

**“I like the way your smile shows up before you laugh.”**

Or

**“There’s something about how relaxed you get when you’re comfortable that keeps pulling me in.”**

Or

**“You have a way of looking at me that makes me forget what I was saying.”**

Those lines don’t scan a body.

They **discover a person.**

And discovery creates tension.

Tension creates fascination.

#### Which leads to the strangest truth in modern attraction.

The more people sexualize themselves to become desirable… the more they destroy the mystery that makes desire possible.

Sexualization doesn’t make someone irresistible.

It makes them **immediately understandable.**

And erotic fascination does not run on understanding.

It runs on discovery.
