**Most of you have never been wanted.**

You’ve been *kept f*or what you did to somebody’s inner world.

For the calm you brought. For the attention you gave. For the warmth you performed. For the way you made loneliness stop screaming for five minutes.

That’s why most romance doesn’t break later. It breaks **at first contact**.

Because the first thing people ask—without saying it—is:

*What do you do for me?*

*What do you soothe?*

*What ache do you quiet?*

*What do you stabilize so I don’t have to face myself?*

*What do you bring to the table?*

And if the answer is “enough,” you’re chosen. If it isn’t, you’re replaced.

That isn’t love. That’s an intake form.

---

Because if somebody actually wanted you, they wouldn’t need you to perform.

They wouldn’t require a constant stream of reassurance.

They wouldn’t need you to manage their moods.

They wouldn’t punish you for having an off day.

They wouldn’t disappear when you stop entertaining them.

Want doesn’t need you to keep proving you’re valuable. Want is what stays when nothing is being produced.

But most of you have never been met there.

So you confuse **being useful** with being loved. You confuse **intensity** with intimacy. You confuse **chemistry** with recognition. You confuse **someone needing you** with someone actually seeing you.

And because that illusion keeps you warm, you don’t realize you’re freezing.

---

This is why so many of you start relationships underwater.

Because the relationship begins with: fixing, over-giving, being “good at love”, managing the vibe, performing safety, holding somebody else’s nervous system together.

And you will call that commitment.

No—listen to me.

If repair is needed immediately, it means the bond was never built on want. It was built on function.

You were hired. Not held.

---

#### And that’s why “fixing” kills intimacy.

Because every time you fix somebody, what you’re really saying is: “I don’t trust you with yourself.”

You’re not loving them. You’re containing them. Turning aching into an argument over authority.

You’re extracting effects: the effect of being needed, the effect of being good, the effect of control, the effect of safety.

And calling it devotion.

But the moment they stabilize without you—or the moment you stop over-functioning, the relationship shakes.

Because it wasn’t you they were attached to. It was the role.

---

And some of y’all are still clinging to the prettiest lie of all:

**“At least I was wanted for my beauty.”**

No.

***You were wanted for the effect your beauty produced.***

Beauty is an accelerant. It delivers: validation, status, arousal, admiration, social proof.

That’s not being wanted. That’s being cast.

Because if aging, grief, illness, boredom, silence—if any of that threatens the bond?

Then it was never want. It was benefit attached to appearance.

Let me make the whole scam plain.

This is what masquerades as love:

- Chemistry
- Intensity
- Affection
- Reassurance
- Consistency
- Sexual desire
- Admiration
- Being needed
- Being chosen
- Being beautiful
- Being helpful.

Those aren’t love. Those are **states.**

And states can be transferred. Replaced. Upgraded. Withdrawn. At any point.

That’s why they don’t survive truth.

Because a real relationship should increase self-honesty—but self-honesty exposes fractures. Those fractures reduce wanting capacity. So honesty punishes the relationship. And the relationship punishes honesty.

That’s the trap.

#### So what is repair?

Repair is not “talking it through.” Repair is not reassurance. Repair is not compromise. Repair is not effort.

Repair is **withdrawing performance without withdrawing presence.**

It’s letting disappointment exist without rushing to soothe it. Letting fear sit without treating it like sound like some kind of emergency. Letting someone face themselves without you buffering the impact.

And that terrifies people—because when you stop performing, they finally feel the emptiness they’ve been running from.

So they call you cold. They call you distant. They call you selfish.

When really… you just stopped feeding the illusion.

---

Want is not a feeling. Want is a capacity.

Wanting someone requires:

- Seeing them without reshaping them.
- Letting them disappoint you without controlling the fallout.
- Staying present without extracting reassurance.
- Wanting their existence even when it produces nothing for you.

That’s not romance culture. That’s adulthood.

And most people can’t do it—because most people were never wanted first.

So no, you’re not broken for wanting to be wanted.

But you will never get there by performing love.

You get there by surviving the moment you stop being useful… that will take half of you to love immediately.

And finding out who can still see you on the other side of being a tool.

That’s repair.

That’s reality.

And yes—the room gets dark when you stop being a tool for others.

You have to love people for the life they live.

Because the illusion of love can not exist in the same space as truth of love.
