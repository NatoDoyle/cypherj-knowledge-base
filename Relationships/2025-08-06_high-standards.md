You don’t have high standards.  
You have abandonment issues with an application process.

**And truthfully?**  
You don’t even want someone to pass the test.  
You want proof that no one ever could.  
Because if no one makes it through, you never have to face what happened the last time you let someone in.

These aren’t boundaries.  
They’re booby traps with velvet signage.  
You don’t want a relationship.  
You want a controlled demolition with mutual attraction.

You built a castle, but every window is an exit strategy, and every drawbridge comes with a psychological NDA:

“Don’t disappoint me like the others did or I’ll become who they warned you about.”

You say you’re protecting your peace but it’s not peace you’re keeping.  
It’s *power*.  
Because power doesn’t leave first.  
Power doesn’t beg.  
Power doesn’t get caught hoping someone meant it this time.

You didn’t raise the bar.  
You raised the walls.  
And now you’re sitting in a fortress designed to punish the one who left  
by never letting anyone else in.

> You call it “**standards**” because “**trust collapse**” sounds too honest.

You call it “knowing your worth,” but worth doesn’t feel this heavy to prove.  
**And it doesn’t require witnesses.**

You’re not filtering out the wrong ones.  
You’re rehearsing your own rejection in reverse.  
Prepping the excuses before anyone even gets close.  
Because if they never make it through the front door, then you never have to find out if they would’ve left the back.

You flinch when love shows up easy.  
You get suspicious when someone doesn’t try too hard.  
Because you’ve mistaken effort for safety and burnout for proof.

You don’t want a partner.  
**You want a prosecutor you can finally out-argue**.

---

You want someone to work so hard to stay  
that it makes your inner child believe they were always worth it.

But love isn’t a courtroom.  
And no one is on trial but you.  
And every “standard” you raise to test them is just another *mirror* for your own fear of being seen unfinished.

You’re not intimidating.  
You’re grieving.  
And you got really good at calling that “preference.”

---

You’re allowed to want more.  
But don’t forget to name what the “more” is for:  
Is it to invite in something sacred?  
Or just to keep yourself safe from trying again?

Healing doesn’t mean lowering your standards.  
It means being honest about who built them and what they were protecting.

> Because “high standards” don’t always make you wise.  
> Sometimes they just mean *you got hurt early and built a resume around it*.

And if you ever want to leave that castle, you’ll have to do something braver than raise the bar.

You’ll have to trust again. And it’s okay if that comes uneasy.  
Because even if your hands shake at the handle you’re still holding it.
