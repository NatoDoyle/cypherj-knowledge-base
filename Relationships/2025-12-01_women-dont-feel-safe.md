> “**Women don’t feel safe**” doesn’t mean what you think it means.

It doesn’t mean she’s judging you but it does mean her body is older than your intentions.

Her fear is not a verdict and your shame is not evidence, but **they collide just like they are.**

So I’m going to really help you:

Because when a woman feels unsafe, **she’s not remembering you**. But she is remembering the world. Her body reacts with every story it ever absorbed long before she met you.

And when a man hears it, he isn’t reacting to her. He’s reacting to the fear that one moment could erase the man he believes he is.

This is two people, same sentence, two completely different survival systems firing.

---

Women don’t feel safe because danger lives in their body.

Men don’t feel safe because accusation lives in their mind.

One is mortal. One is existential.

> **Both feel like death.**

A woman hears the phrase and her chest fills with the faces of women who didn’t get to finish the night.

When a man hears the phrase and his chest fills with all the versions of himself he hopes he never becomes.

So she’s scared of what could happen. You’re scared of what could be said.

She’s protecting **her life.** You’re protecting **your story.**

Neither one is wrong. Neither one is the enemy. But both are terrified of being misread.

---

Because women don’t feel safe not because men are monsters, but because the margin of error is **razor-thin** and the cost of trusting the wrong moment is paid **in her flesh.**

Men feel attacked not because women are blaming them, but because their innocence is fragile and the fear of being mistaken for danger feels like losing your face in the mirror.

SO IT IS EXTREMELY CLEAR.

> Her body is guarding her future. Your ego is guarding your identity. And every conversation about safety is both of you trying not to disappear in completely different ways.

**So** **a woman’s fear isn’t about you but a man’s shame is always about him.**

You are not the man she’s remembering. She is not the jury you’re defending yourself from.

You are here. She is here.

So the real equation you always need to ask yourself when it comes down to this:

1. **Can you hear her body without assuming she’s naming your soul? Regardless of what it says.**
2. **Can she trust your presence without gambling her life?**

If both answers are yes, safety can finally begin.

Not because fear is gone. Not because guilt is gone.

But because the ghosts stopped talking and the humans finally showed up.

I don’t want you to flinch at the reality, it is true women don’t feel safe.

**Achieving** **safety is romance.**

To many people find this negotiable.

It is not negotiable.
