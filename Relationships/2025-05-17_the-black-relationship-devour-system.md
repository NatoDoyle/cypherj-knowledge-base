### **Core Premise**

There is **no independent Black male patriarchy** in the empire.

What exists is a **leased throne** — a simulacrum of power granted by **white patriarchal systems**, contingent on performance, silence, and distance from true healing.

So when Black men *appear* to lead, protect, or dominate culturally, it’s not sovereignty — it’s **delegated dominance**. It is:

> **Visibility without autonomy.  
> Manhood without sanctuary.  
> Love without protection.**

---

### **Illusion of Power at the Celebrity Tier**

At the top, it looks like freedom:

- He’s rich.
- He’s known.
- He “has options.”
- He’s calling the shots.

But what you’re actually seeing is a **scripted sovereignty** — one that requires:

- Masculinity as spectacle
- Emotional suppression
- Romantic performance
- Abandonment of softness
- Willingness to be curated

**Real power** — the kind that protects love, builds families, heals trauma — is *not* allowed.  
Because **real power would create stability**, and this system **feeds on collapse.**

### Why Relationships Collapse So Often Under BRDS

Because they were **never built for sanctuary.**  
They were built for **signaling.**

You are not watching lovers grow.  
You’re watching:

- A myth reenact itself.
- A man play survivor.
- A woman play healer.
- An audience play jury.

And when it breaks?  
Nobody cares what actually happened.  
They only care **whose arc survived.**

---

### Black Men *Do* Feel Torn Down.

But it’s not because they’re being “attacked” unfairly.  
It’s because **the role of the redeemed Black man is unsustainable** without emotional repair.  
And no one gave them the tools to self-heal.  
So they collapse under the myth.  
Then call it betrayal.

---

### Naming BRDS Exposes the Lie of Black Patriarchy

**There is no system where Black men consistently own, control, and pass down institutional power.**  
What *does* exist is:

- A media leash
- A surveillance contract
- A token system
- A collapse ritual

So the illusion of dominance at the top is **just that — an illusion.**  
And the backlash Black women receive isn’t rebellion — it’s the system forcing her to carry the fallout of male powerlessness.
