A lot of people would say they have a type, but most people don’t have a type.

## They have a **performance budget.**

You didn’t discover some sacred biological preference.

You didn’t decode your soul’s romantic blueprint.

You learned how to read **desirability packaging like everybody else.**

That’s it.

You memorized the hair-and-makeup cookbook.

You memorized the lighting.

The outfit language.

The body-presentation signals that whisper:

“I understand the market.”

The instagram-coded clothing.

And the second someone walks in wearing the full presentation kit you go:

*“Damn. That’s my type.”*

No.

That’s your **consumer recognition system.**

Because watch what happens the moment the costume comes off.

- Hair tied back.
- No makeup.
- Sweatpants.
- Just a human being standing there… breathing.

And suddenly your brain glitches.

*“Hmmm… I don’t know if he’s really my type.”*

Exactly.

Because you didn’t pick a person.

You picked a **presentation tier.**

---

### Types let people pretend attraction is destiny.

Like your DNA sat down one day and said:

> “You know what I require? Winged eyeliner and a curated waistline.”

But most of the time we can call it what it actually is.

**Aesthetic classism with hormones.**

You didn’t discover your preference.

**You discovered the social costume that signals desirability capital.**

And the reason the attraction starts negotiating the moment the performance budget drops **is because the signal disappeared.**

Not the person.

The **signal.**

Which means the thing you were responding to wasn’t them.

It was the **packaging language.**

You weren’t looking at a human being.

You were reading a **label.**

And the moment real encounter starts replacing spectacle?

Your type list starts shaking like cheap furniture in an earthquake.

Because singular attraction doesn’t care about templates.

When someone actually gets under your skin…

categories start failing.

The hairstyle stops mattering.

The aesthetic stops mattering.

The body blueprint you swore was your “type” stops mattering.

Because attraction just moved from **display recognition** to **encounter.**

And encounter ruins shopping.

That’s why people cling to types so hard.

Types keep attraction safe.

Types mean you always know what you’re looking for.

---

### But encounter?

Encounter means someone real might rearrange you.

And that is terrifying for people who built their entire romantic identity around **categories.**

Most people don’t have a type.

They have a **presentation addiction.**

And the moment someone real walks in that entire system starts rattling like a dollar-store bookshelf.
