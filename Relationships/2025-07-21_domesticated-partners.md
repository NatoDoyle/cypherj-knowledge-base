*You’re not in love—you’re just scared to be homeless again.”*

They call it a relationship.  
But you showed up already bent.  
Already housebroken.  
Already trained not to speak when it matters.  
Because somewhere along the line **you mistook flinching for loyalty.**

---

You don’t speak your needs.  
You translate them into favors.  
You don’t say “this hurts me.”  
You just hurt quieter.  
You don’t want too much you just learned that wanting anything at all makes you *unwelcome.*

You weren’t taught love—**you were taught not to trigger someone else’s explosion.**

---

And now they call you a “domestic partner.”  
But what you really are… is someone who was **domesticated by trauma.**  
You learned to fold your voice like linens.  
To keep the peace by disappearing into their expectations.

**“You think you’re easy to love. But really you’re just hard to disappoint.”**

You love like someone always one sentence away from eviction.  
You don’t want to be chosen—you want to be safe.  
You want to be loved without risking *being left.*

And that’s not your fault.  
That’s what happens when your nervous system has been trained to believe **that emotional homelessness is worse than being unseen.**

---

You adapt to their trauma.  
You orbit their moods.  
You confuse endurance with commitment.  
And you call it love—even though it feels more like *managing a crisis.*

---

> “You’re not their partner— you’re their emotional tenant. And the rent is paid in silence.”

---

So let’s roll the tape.

- If you say what you really feel will the house collapse?
- If you express your grief will they leave you in it?
- If you name the ache will they still stay?

If the answer is no…  
**You don’t live with a person.**  
**You live inside their survival pattern.**

---

And I know you’ve been told that’s what love is.  
Staying.  
Adjusting.  
Never being too loud about your suffering.

But **love doesn’t require domestication.**  
Love doesn’t shrink you down so someone else feels **bigger**.

Love *remembers you* even when you forget yourself.
