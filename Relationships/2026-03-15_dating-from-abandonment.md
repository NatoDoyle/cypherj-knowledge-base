Dating from abandonment is one of the hardest things you could ever go through with someone.

There’s just a difference between dating someone and bracing against them.

But you walked into the relationship already leaning backward. Not because you don’t care.

Because somewhere inside you is a quiet memory that says:

> **People leave.**

It makes sense every time you hear it.

So you sit across from someone you like already calculating the ending.

Not consciously.

But watch how your mind moves.

- You don’t ask: **“Who are they?”**
- You ask: **“How do I make this work?”**

And that sounds like love.

But it isn’t.

It’s strategy.

Because when you’re dating from abandonment, the relationship isn’t a meeting between two lives.

### It’s an attempt to finally solve something that happened to you before.

So you adapt.

You become better.

Better communicator. Better listener. Better partner.

You read all the books. You learn the language of feelings. You learn how to navigate conflict without raising your voice.

You become the person who can explain everything calmly.

And everyone tells you that’s healthy, right.

But something strange keeps happening.

Even when you do everything right… some of those relationships still collapse.

Not because you weren’t skilled.

Because the person sitting across from you was never actually aligned with your life.

> **And communication cannot manufacture alignment.**

So the brain tries another strategy.

Maybe it’s not performance.

Maybe it’s control.

So you watch more carefully.

You test more carefully.

You ask the questions earlier.

You monitor tone.

You monitor distance.

You monitor whether they’re pulling away.

And sometimes anger shows up.

But that anger is rarely about the argument.

It’s about the possibility hiding behind the argument.

The possibility that the argument is the beginning of the exit.

That’s what you’re afraid of.

---

### So the relationship fills with effort.

Effort to communicate. Effort to stabilize. Effort to explain. Effort to make sure nothing goes wrong.

But underneath all that effort is something almost invisible.

A quiet hope the relationship is carrying all along.

A load-bearing hope.

> **“This time it will be different.”**
>
> **A lot of relationships are built on just that.**

This time I’ll say the right things. This time I’ll choose right. This time I’ll adapt enough that the ending never comes.

And here’s where the fracture appears.

Because once you see the structure clearly you will realize something devastating.

Some people aren’t dating to build a relationship.

**They’re dating to defeat abandonment.**

That’s all they’re doing.

Every partner becomes the next attempt.

The next experiment.

The next proof that the wound can finally be solved.

Which is why something else starts happening that people rarely notice.

### Every time you meet someone… you start writing the story immediately.

You imagine the house.

The trips.

The future.

What it’ll be like in ten years.

How your lives might fit together.

And you think you’re dreaming about love.

But if you’re honest…

a lot of that story isn’t about building a life.

It’s about protecting one.

It’s your brain searching for proof that the ending will finally be different.

Proof that this person stays.

Proof that the equation finally resolves.

Because when something painful happens in your life your brain doesn’t store it the way you think it does.

You think it just stored a memory.

But it didn’t.

**It stored a scene without an ending.**

A moment where your emotions showed up and the other person disappeared.

And the brain cannot tolerate unfinished equations.

So it time travels.

It keeps scanning the world looking for the same shape.

Not because it wants pain.

**Because it wants the ending it never got to see.**

So now there are moments the brain stored years ago, still there.

- Distance = no ending.
- Silence = no ending.
- Withdrawal = no ending.
- Uncertainty = no ending.

Every memory saved with the equal sign.

No answer.

Just a problem waiting to be solved.

So when someone appears who resembles the pattern…

#### the brain lights up.

Not because they’re your person.

Because the equation has returned.

Another chance to finish it.

Another chance to prove the ending can finally be different.

Which is why intensity can feel like destiny.

Why unpredictability can feel like chemistry.

Why calm, emotionally healthy people sometimes feel…

quiet.

Flat.

Like something’s missing.

But nothing is missing.

**You’re just not used to relationships that aren’t organized around surviving them.**

And this is why disagreeing feels so existential for you.

---

### Because for some of you disagreement has never been a conversation.

### It’s always been a cliff.

You didn’t grow up watching people argue and stay.

You watched people argue and leave.

Argue and disappear.

Argue and withdraw love.

So when someone disagrees with you now, your body doesn’t hear: “We see this differently.” “We just have differences.”

> Your body hears: “This is the moment everything breaks.”

So guess what happens:

So your chest tightens.

Your voice sharpens.

You start explaining yourself faster.

Trying to say the perfect sentence that keeps the person from walking away.

Or maybe you shut down completely.

Because if you don’t speak, maybe the argument won’t turn into the ending you remember.

And some of the things you tried to say in those moments…

you meant them.

The truths you tried to speak.

The boundaries you tried to draw.

The pain you tried to explain.

But because the moment felt like a cliff you never got to be heard.

**Because you were fighting to keep the relationship alive, not to tell the truth.**

So the words never landed.

And years later you’re still carrying sentences inside your chest that never finish.

---

### So I want you to understand something…

**Stop trying to find the ending to abandonment in another person.**

**I want you to give it up.**

Give abandonment an ending today.

Right now.

I want you to understand here’s how it went.

The ending to abandonment is this:

They left.

And you survived.

And you’re still here.

Your life didn’t disappear.

Your story didn’t end.

The equation was never:

- **Distance = I’m not enough.**
- **Silence = I’m not lovable.**
- **Withdrawal = I failed.**

The real ending was always simpler.

Two lives moved apart.

And one life continued.

That’s all that matters.

Yours.

Not the abandonment.

---

### So let’s go back to the equations.

But this time…

finish it.

**Distance = I kept living.**

**Silence = I kept breathing.**

**Withdrawal = I kept becoming.**

The ending was never abandonment!

**The ending was you survived it.**

And once that equation finally closes…

something incredible unlocks in your body.

You stop looking for people who feel like unfinished stories.

You stop negotiating your entire self just to keep someone beside you.

**You stop dating to defeat abandonment.**

And for the first time…you can finally date to discover something much more honest.

Whether two lives actually belong beside each other.

That matters way more than abandonment.
