*You’re not fighting for unconditional love,  
you’re recovering from how early you learned it wasn’t real.”*

They told you love was unconditional.  
But they gave it to you in rations.  
Smiles when you were quiet.  
Praise when you didn’t cry.  
A door held open, but only if you carried yourself like **furniture**, still, silent, and easy to move.

That wasn’t love.  
That was an audition.  
And you passed.  
But at what cost?

So unconditional love became the myth you had to embody, just to survive someone else's conditions.

---

But let’s also hold it still where we’re aiming at it.

Conditional love is *transactional*.

It says: “I’ll love you… **if**.”  
If you shrink.  
If you soften.  
If you forgive me faster than I fix myself.  
If your boundaries are negotiable.  
If your anger doesn’t make a scene.

It’s a **contract written in disappearing ink.**  
You didn’t even know you signed it but you kept paying the penalty fees with your spirit.

---

Unconditional love?  
It doesn’t say, “There are no standards.”  
It says, “**Your** ***humanity*** **isn’t a dealbreaker**.”  
You get to mess up.  
You get to have a *bad year*, not just a bad moment.  
You get to be *real* without being exiled.

**It doesn’t mean you’ll never disappoint them—**  
**It means they won’t punish you for being human.**

And most people aren’t fighting to *receive* unconditional love—they’re afraid of what will happen when they finally *expect it.*

Because when you've never known anything but punishment…  
peace feels suspicious.  
Acceptance feels like a trick.  
Safety feels like a setup.

You don’t feel loved.  
You feel tolerated.  
And deep down, you’ve called that home.

---

So we can stop pretending.

What people call “unconditional love” in their family?  
Is just *guilt with dinner plans.*  
It’s trauma with a smile.  
It’s generational silence passed down like heirlooms.  
And then wrapped in "**you know your mama tried her best.**"

But survival isn’t the same thing as safety.  
And performing isn’t the same thing as being loved.”

---

If they only stayed because you stayed manageable, **it wasn’t love.**  
If they punished your boundaries, **it wasn’t love.**  
If they confused your endurance with devotion, **that wasn’t your failure.**  
That was their capacity.

---

So no—this isn’t about hating anyone.  
This IS about *naming the illusory effect* they needed you to live inside.  
And then choosing to leave it.

Because the moment you realize unconditional love exists—is the same moment you recognize:

**You’ve never actually been given it.**  
Only trained to survive without it.
