Many men struggle with the exact same thing.

The obsession over **who women choose**.

And I want to say something out loud that a lot of people try to dance around.

## Sometimes women **do pick bad men**.

Not “bad” in the sense of imperfect—everyone’s imperfect.

**I mean genuinely destructive men.**

- Men who cheat.
- Men who lie.
- Men who waste people’s time.
- Men who hurt them.

That happens.

And there are men who have spent **their entire lives** trying to solve that mystery.

> Why do women pick them?
>
> Why does the guy who treats people well get ignored while the guy who causes chaos keeps getting chosen?

**So they start studying it.**

They watch.

They analyze.

They compare.

Who gets attention.

Who gets dates.

Who gets chased.

And before they realize it, attraction has turned into a **crime investigation**.

They’re standing outside the building, staring through the window, trying to reconstruct what happened inside.

But here’s the catastrophic mistake in that whole process.

They’re studying **the outcome**, not **the mechanism**.

They’re studying **who got picked**.

Not **what actually produced the attraction in the moment**.

And the moment you start analyzing attraction through outcomes, your brain builds the wrong model.

It starts to believe women are sitting there running an evaluation system.

Ranking men.

Scoring them.

Handing out selections like judges in a competition.

But that’s not what’s happening most of the time.

- **Women don’t primarily respond to evaluation, telling a woman that is a good man will not get him chosen.**
- **Telling a man a woman is faithful will not suddenly make him attracted to her.**

## They respond to **vitality**.

Presence.

Energy.

Confidence that’s alive instead of performed.

Humor.

Unpredictability.

A man who actually feels like he’s **inside** his own life.

And here’s the uncomfortable part.

#### Vitality and goodness are **not the same thing**.

- A man can be kind, responsible, moral, loyal—and still feel emotionally flat, cautious, hesitant, contained.
- Another man can be reckless, immature, chaotic—and still carry a kind of aliveness that pulls attention in a room.

That doesn’t make the second man better.

It just means attraction isn’t a **moral reward system**.

It’s a **vitality response**.

And once you understand that, the entire obsession with “who women choose” starts collapsing.

Because that question is built on a hidden assumption:

That attraction should behave like **justice**.

That the good man should automatically win.

But attraction isn’t justice.

It’s **energy meeting energy in real time**.

Which means if you spend your life staring at the scoreboard—who women chose, who women rejected—you will never actually understand the game.

You’ll just keep building theories about the winners which ironically keeps you watch their podcast.

While the real mechanism of attraction keeps happening somewhere else entirely.

Inside the moment.

Inside interaction.

Inside the people who walk into a room actually **alive enough to move it**.
