They told you there’s *One.*

One person.

One job.

One chance.

One dream.

One Church you had to filter God through.

One family you couldn’t disappoint.

One platform to prove your worth.

And if you miss it?

**You’re nothing.**

---

> *But the One was never destiny. It was the first cage you were handed.*

---

They branded scarcity as sacred.

They told you you’d be completed if you found it, and destroyed if you lost it, didn’t they.

But that was never intimacy.

Not calling.  
Not freedom.

That was captivity rehearsed in every language you’d trust.

Let’s go through them.

---

- **The One Person?**  
  If they were half of you, you’d already be dead.

  If they were your air, every argument with them would be suffocation.

  If they were your destiny, their absence would be execution.
- **The One Job?**  
  If one paycheck proves you exist, then unemployment is genocide.
- **The One Dream?**  
  If one failure means you vanish, then your life was never more than a dice roll.
- **The One Church?**  
  If presence needs a building to be real, then you were sold walls, not God.
- **The One Family?**  
  If blood was the only bond, you’d drown in it before you ever tasted love.
- **The One Platform?**  
  If clicks are your mirror, you’ll die of thirst drinking your own reflection before you ever reach water.

---

*Every “One” was just another ransom note pinned to your chest.*

---

And the chemicals—they rewired those too.

**Dopamine**, meant to pull you toward joy, is now a countdown clock in your spine.

**Cortisol**, meant to warn of danger, is now the barrel pressed to your temple.

**Oxytocin**, meant to bond you in trust, is now the rope biting into your wrists.

Let’s keep going:

**Serotonin**, meant to steady your peace, is now the red dot across your forehead.

**Endorphins**, meant to soothe your pain, are now the false skylight painted above your cell.

## *They didn’t just hijack your love life. They hijacked your nervous system.*

But listen to me.

The One was never out there.

The One was always you.

And they knew if you rested in that, their whole economy would collapse.

So they taught you to fall in love with your reflection in the world instead of resting in where you’re already held.

*You were not born split.*

## *You were born whole, and sold scarcity as salvation.*

---

What’s out there is bigger than “the one.”

- It’s the freedom to be held without begging for safety.
- It’s the freedom to belong without proof.
- It’s the freedom to ache without turning it into ransom.

---

***You were never waiting for The One.***

That’s I’m trying to say.

*You were waiting to stop mistaking captivity for love.*

Look in the mirror and see The One. **Look through it.**

If you really want to find it. The One is what you had to give to the world.

You were *The One.*
