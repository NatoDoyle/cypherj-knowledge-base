“Let’s be real:  
Most people say they want commitment…  
but they’re just trying to *trap a relationship in a contract.*  
They want the *promise of forever*  
because they’re terrified of being alone tomorrow.  
That’s not devotion.  
That’s fear management.”

---

“See, a relationship is an *experience.*  
But commitment? That’s a *practice.*  
One is about connection.  
The other is about *continuity*.  
And some of y’all are trying to commit to someone you’ve never even been fully *real* with.  
You perform love, but hide your grief.  
You show up daily, but never *let them in.*  
You’ve got shared bills, shared goals—  
but not a shared soul.”

---

“Commitment isn’t *'we stay no matter what.’*  
It’s *'we grow, no matter what it reveals.’*  
It’s not a trap door labeled 'forever.’  
It’s a fire you tend *daily*,  
because you *want to*, not because you signed up to suffer.  
And some of y’all confuse *consistency* with *connection.*  
You’re showing up…  
but are you *still arriving?*”

---

“Commitment without presence is a relic.  
It’s what our ancestors *survived* in-  
not what they dreamed for us.  
They didn’t fight to keep love trapped.  
They fought to make love *free and true.*  
So stop trying to force people to stay  
when the staying has no soul in it.  
Freedom is not the *opposite* of commitment.  
It’s the *proof* of it.”

---

“Your partner doesn’t want to be *owned.*  
They want to be *witnessed.*  
They want to be chosen again and again-  
not just remembered as a choice you made once.  
Commitment isn’t about being locked in.  
It’s about being *lit from within.*  
And if the fire’s out…  
ask what you stopped feeding it with.”

---

“Relationship says: I want you.  
Commitment says: I’ll meet you—even when I’m scared.  
So don’t ask if they’ll stay.  
Ask if they’re still *becoming* with you.  
That’s the real vow.”
