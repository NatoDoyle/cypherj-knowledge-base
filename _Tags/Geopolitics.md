# Geopolitics

## Justin's Perspective

Scott approaches geopolitics not as a map of nation-states but as a set of replicable templates for control. Gaza becomes "the Gaza Method" — an operating system of algorithmic occupation where atrocity is A/B tested for optics, infrastructure functions as leash, and peace is rebranded silence. Venezuela's boat-strike corridor reveals the same logic domestically: pre-borders disguised as policing, movement controlled before people reach legal jurisdiction. He traces a consistent pipeline across Sudan, the DRC, and Mali where conflict-zone gold flows through Dubai and Swiss refineries into global finance, exposing "Free Sudan" sloganeering as hollow without demanding the ledger. In his Iran analysis, Scott identifies the psychological front as the primary battlefield for American citizens — foreign ambiguity compresses into binary loyalty, which enables emergency enforcement and normalized expansion of authority. His recurring argument is that fascism does not arrive marching; it arrives "wearing a disaster badge," administering through paperwork, budgets, and narrative alignment rather than dramatic crackdowns.
