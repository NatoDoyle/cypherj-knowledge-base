### **Don’t Mistake Early Energy for Victory**

When a city rises — when the streets flood, windows break, voices thunder —  
it *feels* like we’re winning.

But remember this:  
**Surveillance sees crowds. The state sees patterns.**  
The algorithm sees *content*.

Your moment of rage can become someone else’s marketing campaign.  
Or worse — your downfall.

> “They don’t need to beat the movement, just *brand it before you do*.”

**Early energy** is a match.  
**Victory** is the controlled burn.

---

### **Channel Momentum Into Strategy**

Right now, in LA, the rage is real. The injustice is real. That’s okay…  
But **who's documenting the demands?**  
Who’s capturing the **why** of it before the media invents their version?

**Narrative Warfare means we write the myth** ***before*** **they assign the blame.**

Tactics:

- **Document with precision.** Every chant, every action — frame it with clarity.
- **Avoid lone heroes.** They’ll target one name. Build networks.
- **Assign roles.** Protestor. Witness. Healer. Strategist. Recorder. Protector.
- **Protect your narrative command center** like it’s your life’s work — because it is.

> **"Every camera pointed at you is a potential weapon or a weapon you take back."**

---

### **Avoid Provocation or Reactionary Escalation**

This is **textbook narrative warfare**:

1. **Embed agents** to throw the first Molotov.
2. **Film it.**
3. **Upload it.**
4. **Let America write the ending.**

**Don’t take the bait.**

Every overreaction becomes a headline.  
Every misstep becomes justification.  
Every act of community defense will be framed as “chaos” if you don’t own the frame first.

**“They want the image of the riot, not the reason for the rage.”**

---

### **How to Use Narrative Warfare for** ***Us***:

1. **Set the stage before the media gets there.**

   - Have your storytellers *ready.* On the ground. Online. In the DMs.
2. **Use emotional truth to inoculate your audience.**

   - Say the names. Show the faces. Teach the history. Anchor the moment.
3. **Pre-label state responses.**

   - If you know they’ll call it looting, call it **“extractive grief”** first.
   - If you know they’ll claim “safety,” define it as **“militarized peace.”**
4. **Control the rhythm.**

   - Don’t speak only in response.
   - Issue your *own briefings, your own visuals, your own declarations.*

**“When the people write the truth, the state has to fight the lie.”**

---

> **“This is a moment — not a movement. Unless you shape the story, it dies in headlines and hashtags.”**
