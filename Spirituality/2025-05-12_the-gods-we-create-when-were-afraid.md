You know that moment when the God you were taught about doesn't match what you feel in your bones? That disconnect isn't accidental—it's the shadow of projection.

What if our conceptions of "God" are actually reflections of our collective trauma, cultural inheritance, and desperate attempts at control?

## When Deliverance Comes With Conditions

In the Black Church tradition, particularly post-slavery and under systemic oppression, a powerful divine projection emerged—one I call **The Delivering Judge**:

> *"God will break your chains—but only if you're holy enough to be chosen."*

This isn't just theology—it's a survival adaptation born from genocide and dehumanization. The Black Church forged a God who could both liberate and protect, but this hybrid deity carries mixed messages:

- "Suffering is refinement"
- "Holiness is separation"
- "Deliverance must be earned"

When worship becomes performance, you begin to:

- Confuse fear with reverence
- Believe liberation comes only through suffering
- Fear your own emotions might threaten your salvation
- Treat joy as reward rather than birthright

The tragedy? **The Spirit survived despite these projections**. Beneath doctrine, in the moans, harmonies, and shouts, existed what I call **The Emberbound**—warm presence that carries grief without explanation.

## The Sky Father and His Siblings

***This is a Generative Image***

Beyond the Black Church lies another dominant projection: **The Throne** or "Sky Father"—God as distant monarch whose authority demands obedience.

But this "god" is not God. This is a trauma-forged sovereign born from:

- Empire wounds
- Patriarchal structures
- Fear of chaos

When you worship The Throne, you:

- Measure your worth by compliance
- Mistake fear for reverence
- Perform holiness while numbing humanity

And The Throne isn't alone. Other divine distortions include:

**The Mirror Sovereign** ("God is who I must become")

- Worship becomes audition
- Your spirituality shatters under judgment

**The Engineer God** ("God is a system of rules")

- Prayer becomes calculation
- Mystery feels threatening

**The Sacred Firestarter** ("God burns what He hates")

- Righteousness becomes warfare
- You confuse aggression for passion

**The Cosmic Absentee** ("God is disinterested")

- You spiritualize depression
- Hope becomes something you mock

**The Overmother** ("God loves everyone, so nothing matters")

- Boundaries disappear
- Truth drowns in false compassion

## What These Projections Cost Us

Each projection forms when a core fear remains unintegrated:

- Fear of abandonment creates distance
- Fear of unworthiness demands performance
- Fear of failure requires perfection
- Fear of complexity forces oversimplification

The more distorted the projection, the more shame you carry just for being human.

## Finding Your Way Back to Presence

The antidote isn't abandoning God—it's discovering Presence that:

- Doesn't demand performance
- Doesn't punish confusion
- Doesn't wait to be impressed
- Breathes through your ache and asks only your return

## The Question That Matters

Which projection speaks in your mind today? And are you ready to let it dissolve not by walking away from God, but by letting God become real again?

Because when you do, you don't just escape a loop of fear. You come home to what has always been true:

> "I was never above you.  
> I have always been with you."
