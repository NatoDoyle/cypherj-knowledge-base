"Nobody deserves to die." That's the chant.

The phrase whispered at gravesides, the shield held up in mourning, the lullaby we teach to quiet the ache.

And I don't deny the ache. But ache is not the same as truth.

## "They had a family."

Yes. So did Pharaoh.

So did every general who marched nations into graves.

Collapse does not pause because someone at home loves you. That's not how world logic works.

If you weld your body to a collapsing system, you don't get to hide behind your children. Your allegiance becomes their inheritance. And collapse does not ask permission before it tears through bloodlines.

Every empire that fell was full of families. Every oppressor was someone's father. Collapse does not discriminate. It unravels whatever is sewn into its fabric.

## "They were misunderstood."

No. Distortion is not a mistranslation. Collapse is not a word problem. They weren't misunderstood. They were committed. Their body carried the system.

## "They could've changed."

Maybe. But possibility is not presence. Change is not a ghost, it is a step. And until the step is taken, they remain fabric sewn to distortion. The veil doesn't wait for what-ifs. The tear comes. And fabric unravels.

## "But look at the good they did."

Pharaoh built monuments. Rome built roads. Every empire hid scraps of good in its teeth. But a smile doesn't cancel the blood beneath it. Collapse isn't paused by a résumé.

## "They didn't know better."

Ignorance can excuse error. But not persistence. Not when the warnings stood in front of them, and they still welded their breath to distortion. At some point, not knowing becomes not caring. And not caring becomes collapse.

## "God wouldn't have wanted this."

Scripture disagrees.

- Egypt's firstborn.
- Korah swallowed alive.
- Ananias and Sapphira falling at Peter's feet.

The Bible does not hide collapse. It records it. Not as murder, but as consequence. Not as cruelty, but as tearing.

So no, **family, misunderstanding, possibility, charity, ignorance, even God's supposed reluctance**, none of these undo the consequence of welding your body to distortion. None of these SAVE YOU.

From personal collapse, political collapse, relational, etc.

The veil tears. And the fabric unravels.
