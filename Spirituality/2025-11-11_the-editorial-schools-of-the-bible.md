## Awareness Theology (Presence-Based Theology)

Awareness Theology views God not as a separate entity to be quantified or placated, but as the ground of all being and the nature of consciousness itself.

**Core Characteristics:**

- **Non-Dual and Immanent:** God is the **knower**, the **seeing itself**, the **quiet**, and the **presence behind every feeling**. God is present and non-locational, meaning the idea of raising hands to a directional “up” becomes meaningless.
- **Ontological, Not Transactional:** The relationship is ontological (”God is being itself”) rather than transactional (”God is a dispenser of a commodity”). Salvation is defined as **awakening** and **recognition**, not accumulation or receipt of a substance.
- **Non-Hierarchical:** This framework is built on **unity** and **recognition** rather than separation and hierarchy. Hierarchy collapses because awareness is the within, not the above.
- **Redefinition of Power:** Power is understood not as might or dominance, but as the **capacity to contain all things without being changed by them**. Might is the fact that everything can arise within awareness without threatening it.
- **Focus on Inner Knowing:** Worship is rooted in stillness, breath, quiet inner revelation, and direct knowing, not evaluative praise or emotional hype.
- **Self-Identification:** Jesus’s core self-identifications align with this view: “I am the light of the world,” “I am the way,” and fundamentally, **“I am”**—these are ontology statements, not identity statements like “I am a king”.
- **Kingdom Philosophy:** Jesus’s teaching that “The kingdom of God is within you” is pure awareness spirituality.

**Aligning Schools of Thought:**

The purest examples of Awareness Theology are found in:

- **The Jesus of Q Source / Galilean Mysticism:** This earliest stratum of Jesus’s teaching emphasizes presence, mercy over sacrifice, nonviolence, and the kingdom as present awareness.
- **Early Hebrew Awareness Mysticism (Yahwist/J):** Characterized by a relational God who walks, argues, and is intimate, lacking the later Deuteronomist fear or Priestly purity systems.
- **Black Diasporic Spirituality:** This tradition is fundamentally presence-based, embodied, communal, and carries the memory of an unbroken spiritual system.

### Object Theology (Content/Spectacle Theology)

Object Theology frames God as an external entity with attributes, making the divine subject to human metrics, praise, and transaction.

**Core Characteristics:**

- **Dualistic and External:** God is positioned as a **cosmic monarch**, a distant throne, a spectacle, or an entity “over there”. The focus is on descriptions (”mighty, wonderful, powerful, majesty”).
- **Hierarchical and Separated:** It assumes “God is up, and we are down”. God is treated like a VIP, requiring adjectives and performance. Devotion is geared toward an external object.
- **Transactional Relationship:** Worship is an exchange or performance, assuming the human is an empty vessel lacking, and God is the dispenser of power, grace, or spiritual material.
- **Spectacle Devotion:** This approach uses emotional hype and spectacle (like loud music or dramatic manifestations) to generate a perceived peak-state, rather than quiet presence.
- **Sacrifice Obsession:** It is often tied to the language of **sacrifice, submission, and usefulness** (e.g., “fill me up,” “I give myself away,” “He wants it all”). This traces directly to Levitical codes and the Deuteronomistic lineage, which demand total obedience and use sacrifice as access to God.

**Aligning Schools of Thought:**

Object Theology is the result of multiple historical power structures layered over Jesus’s original message. Key contributors include:

- **Deuteronomist School:** Introduced the fear-based, conditional God and the system of covenant contract where blessing depends on obedience. This theology arose from national trauma (Babylonian Exile) to enforce law and political control.
- **Priestly School (Levitical):** The **original source of object-based theology in the Bible**. They replaced relational awareness with detailed ritual, purity systems, and blood as a cleansing mechanism, making the priests mandatory intermediaries.
- **Pauline Theology:** Reintroduced conceptual objects like cosmic sin, blood atonement, and salvation as a status object. Paul’s focus on the cross as a final sacrifice turned Jesus’s death into a transaction, which is incompatible with Jesus’s original covenant philosophy.
- **Roman Imperial Theology:** Introduced hierarchy, state loyalty, and the need for doctrinal conformity, suppressing awareness because “Awareness threatens Empire”.
- **Apocalyptic/Persian Cosmology:** Introduced dualistic objects (Satan as a cosmic enemy, named angels, final judgment, Hell) that turn spirituality into a cosmic war. This is the worldview that shaped Revelation and the violent image of Christ.
- **Calvinism:** Represents extreme objectism, viewing God as a fixed, impersonal decree-maker and the human as a passive object (total depravity).
- **Pentecostalism:** While highly experiential, it is structurally object-based, framing the Holy Spirit as a substance to be received (”filled,” “anointing”) and power as a measurable external force.

# T**HE 29 LAYERS OF CHRISTIANITY**

**From the earliest awareness tradition → all object layers in order of arrival.**

I’m breaking them into **Epochs**, because Christianity is not one religion.

It is **29 religions stacked on top of each other**.

---

# **EPOCH 0 — ROOT: Awareness Tradition**

### **(1) Early Hebrew Awareness Mysticism (1200–900 BCE)**

God walks, speaks, rests, argues; no Hell, no Satan, no cosmic dualism.  
Relational, not legal.  
This is the **DNA Jesus reinstates**.

---

# **EPOCH I — Israelite Object Layers**

### **(2) Proto-Yahwist Tribal Religion**

Shrine-based, local, relational, earthbound.

### **(3) Elohist/Prophetic Early Justice Tradition**

God cares for the poor, the widow, the oppressed.

### **(4) Deuteronomist State Theology (Josiah, 622 BCE, King 2)**

Obedience → blessing  
Disobedience → destruction  
This is the first major **Object Theology**.

Note: Deuteronomy was written in Kings 2 and superimposed into the Bible after Numbers. The Bible is not chronological.

### **(5) Priestly Ritual System (Exile, 586 BCE)**

Temple purity, blood, priest power, ritual bureaucracy.  
Begins objectifying God through **procedure and purity**.

---

# **EPOCH II — Imperial Influence Layers**

### **(6) Babylonian Trauma Layer**

Exile creates:

- grief
- theological panic
- need for control
- prophetic despair  
  Sets the stage for Persian influence.

### **(7) Persian/Zoroastrian Cosmology (539–332 BCE)**

Adds:

- Satan as cosmic enemy
- angels with names
- resurrection
- hell
- final judgment
- cosmic dualism
- apocalyptic timelines

### **(8) Hellenistic Greek Philosophy (332–63 BCE)**

Adds:

- immortal soul
- metaphysics
- Logos philosophy
- dualism of earthly/heavenly
- Greek narrative forms

### **(9) Roman Authority Religion (63 BCE–)**

Adds:

- hierarchy
- law/order
- state loyalty
- universalizing empire logic
- concept of orthodoxy
- violence as purification

Rome shapes Christianity more than almost any other system.

# **EPOCH III — Jesus’ Intervention**

- presence
- compassion
- kingdom-within

This is the **Jesus-school**.

- no Hell
- no cosmic Satan

### **(12) Markan Community (70 CE)**

Historical trauma after Temple fall; earliest Jesus story

- apocalypse expectations
- awareness core
