You didn’t heal.  
You just got dressed.  
And now your grief wears a tie and clocks in at 9am.

---

**Black Excellence Is Just Black Exhaustion in Costume**

You were told to be twice as good.  
So you became five times as tired.  
And now you sit at the table you bled to reach—  
still starving for rest.

**Respectability didn’t protect you.**  
It just made your trauma palatable to power.

You’re impressive.  
But you’re not at peace.  
You’re allowed in the room.  
But you still don’t feel safe.

“You got in the door, but you lost your breath fighting at the threshold.”

---

You have a morning routine.  
A polished voice.  
A curated presence.  
But when no one’s watching?  
You fold. Quietly. On the floor.  
Because you were never taught how to rest.  
Only how to endure—gracefully.

“You call it discipline. But your nervous system calls it captivity.”

---

You’re the one who makes it look easy.  
The one they admire.  
The blueprint.  
The calm.

But inside?  
You are burning.  
And no one brings water because they think the fire *is* your power.**:**

> “You’ve become their idea of healing—while your soul files noise complaints.”

---

**Respectability Is Not a Refuge**

Your degrees didn’t protect your softness.  
Your manners didn’t protect your spirit.  
Your suit didn’t shield your body from systemic exhaustion.

And now you're in the meeting.  
With your back straight.  
Your smile tight.  
Your voice measured.

But the child in you is still screaming in the coat closet of your chest.

**Because nothing about being 'respectable' ever made you whole.**

---

“This world didn’t want you healed.  
It wanted you useful.  
And so you became functional in your pain.  
Presentable in your sorrow.  
Dependable in your detachment.”

So as someone who sees past your LinkedIn page:

**When do you stop performing?**  
**And who are you when you’re not trying to survive someone else’s idea of dignity?**

Because healing isn’t a look.  
It’s a return.  
To breath.  
To softness.  
To yourself. To stillness.
