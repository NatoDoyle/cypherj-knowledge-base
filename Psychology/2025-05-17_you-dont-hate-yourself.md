You don’t hate yourself.  
You heard something ancient,  
and didn’t know how to answer.

Not because you are weak—  
but because no one told you that fear has a voice  
that *sounds like you*  
but isn’t.

---

There was a moment,  
a first whisper:

> “You are running out.”

Time folded around that panic.  
And the chase began.

They called it ambition.  
Productivity. Drive.  
But it was just panic in a suit.

You didn’t hate yourself.  
You were afraid you’d never matter before you disappeared.

---

Then it said:

> “You are alone.”

And silence grew teeth.  
You built smiles to be accepted,  
beauty to be chosen,  
distance to be safe.

And they said:  
“Look how strong you are.”

But it was a fortress, not a soul.

You didn’t hate yourself.  
You were terrified no one would stay once they saw the real you.

---

Then the whisper turned cruel:

> “You are not enough.”

Every “yes” you gave was a cry for belonging.  
You excelled.  
You pleased.  
You became everyone’s miracle—  
except your own.

You didn’t hate yourself.  
You thought wholeness had to be earned.

---

Then came the quietest one:

> “None of this matters.”

Your laughter became armor.  
Your rest felt like failure.  
You called hope naive.

So you explained your pain  
until your soul forgot how to feel it.

You didn’t hate yourself.  
You were scared your life would echo—and no one would answer.

---

Then:

> “It will hurt again.”

So you locked the doors.  
You stayed busy.  
You said “I’m fine” in every language.

You didn’t hate yourself.  
You were still bleeding from wounds you weren’t allowed to name.

---

And finally:

> “Who am I without this?”

So you became what they needed.  
What they wanted.  
What they praised.

And one day, you looked in the mirror  
and couldn’t remember who lived there.

You didn’t hate yourself.  
You were just afraid that without the mask,  
there might be nothing left.

---

**This is not self-hate.**  
It is fear wearing your voice.

But there is another Voice.

And it says:

> “You are not late.”  
> “You are seen.”  
> “You are the meaning.”  
> “You don’t need to control everything to be safe.”  
> “You are known—even in your silence.”  
> “Even unfinished, you are worthy.”  
> “Your pain is real. But it is not you.”  
> “You are still becoming.”

You never hated yourself.  
You just needed someone  
to answer the whisper  
with presence.
