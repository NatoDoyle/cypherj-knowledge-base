> Let the good woman die.

Because she was never alive just a silhouette men applauded for staying still.

She smiled through a chokehold called “grace.”

She whispered comfort while her throat filled with swallowed truths.

She kept peace that only existed because she kept *quiet.*

## Men said she was rare.

What they meant was *obedient.*

A “good woman” is what’s left after a lifetime of being graded for survival.

- Beautiful, but not *disruptive.*
- Strong, but never *angry.*
- Desirable, but not *desiring.*

She is the world’s favorite ghost!

- Visible, but never seen.

The “good woman” causes harm because she teaches the world that a woman must disappear to be worthy of staying.

She becomes the blueprint for her own erasure—performing love that costs her her voice, performing peace that costs her her sanity.

## Let her die.

Because the whole woman has been banging on the inside of that coffin, begging for air.

The whole woman doesn’t smile to make you comfortable.

She smiles because she chooses joy.

She doesn’t forgive to be holy.

She forgives when it’s safe.

She doesn’t stay quiet to keep peace.

She speaks to make it *real.*

The whole woman is not gentle because she must be, she is gentle because she can be.

Her softness is earned. Her anger is sacred.

Her existence is not an act of service.

Let the good woman die.

Let the whole one rise:

**messy, loud, honest, uncurated.**

The kind of woman who doesn’t just survive the story, she rewrites it.

Because the world doesn’t need another “good woman.”

It needs a woman who finally remembers that she was supposed to die loved…and she was never supposed to die to be loved.
