You woke up today and couldn’t get out of bed.

Not because you’re lazy but because the thought of being seen while broken still makes your chest lock up.

I already know what’s going on.

You tell yourself:

- ”*I’ll go out when I’m healed.”*
- ”*I’ll make friends when I’m ready.”*
- ”*I’ll reach for peace when I’ve earned it.”*

But deep down you know that day **never comes.**

You’ve been living like “unfinished” is a crime.

Like every silence is a test you’re already failing.

Like the world will only let you stay if you keep narrating your worth louder than your fears.

> That’s why your mind won’t shut off at night.
>
> That’s why you rehearse instead of speak.
>
> That’s why every pause feels like danger because you were taught you had to prove yourself before you were allowed to just exist.

**And I want you to understand:**

**Being unfinished does not make you unworthy.**

- You are allowed to sit at the table with your wounds still open.
- You are allowed to rest while the work is unfinished.
- You are allowed to be loved without an explanation.

It’s just true.

In being unfinished, they make you think that completion is human, but in reality incompletion what makes you **here**.

But really unfinished is the only way somebody’s ever shown.

Anybody, your whole life… was through them being seen unfished.

So no—you don’t need to be fixed first.  
You don’t need to explain first.  
You don’t need to prove first.

You are already allowed.

**To sit.  
To rest.  
To belong.**  
Right here, **unfinished.**

And the doorway you’re terrified to walk through?  
The one that says,

*They’ll see me, I’m not ready yet*—

That doorway is the only way forward.
