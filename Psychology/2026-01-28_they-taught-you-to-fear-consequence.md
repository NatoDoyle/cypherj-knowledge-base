Like consequence was cruelty. Like consequence was abuse. Like consequence was something done *to* you.

But consequence was the only proof you were ever real.

Because before consequence, everything you did was just motion. Noise. Performance.

You could say whatever. Do whatever. Touch whoever. Destabilize whatever.

And if nothing came back to you?

It didn’t mean you were free. It meant you were insulated.

Power doesn’t corrupt people. **Consequence removal does.**

The moment your actions stop answering back, you don’t become confident—you become untethered.

And they train you for this early. Quietly. Politely.

They give you phrases.

“I didn’t mean it like that.”

“That wasn’t my intention.”

“You’re taking it the wrong way.”

“That feels unfair.”

All of those sound reasonable. Until you realize what they’re doing.

They’re teaching you how to separate **cause from effect**.

Not to repair harm. To *discount* it.

That’s where entitlement actually starts.

Not in wanting more. In wanting the cost reduced.

---

And this is where it shows up the clearest—**in relationships**.

Because relationships are the one place where consequence is immediate.

Someone pulls away. Someone hardens. Someone stops trusting you. Someone changes how they feel.

And instead of asking, **“What did I cause?”**

You’re taught to ask, **“Why are you reacting like this?”**

That question right there is consequence avoidance in its purest form.

Because now the problem isn’t what you did—it’s that they *felt* it.

Now distance is “punishment.” Boundaries are “control.” Loss of trust is “unfair.”

So you explain. You justify. You defend your intention.

And slowly, without noticing, you start asking for intimacy without accountability.

You want closeness without cost. Forgiveness without metabolization. Connection without consequence.

That’s not love. That’s extraction with feelings.

And the moment someone refuses to absorb it for you—when they pull back, when they change, when they stop offering the same access—

You don’t see it as feedback.

You see it as harm.

That’s the turn.

That’s when accountability becomes “attack.”

Feedback becomes “violence.”

Reality becomes “unsafe.”

And now you can act freely while insisting on innocence.

That’s not freedom. That’s fantasy you’ve enforced on other people.

---

A person who knows consequence exists still lives in the world. They hesitate. They calibrate. They let their actions cost them.

But someone who thinks consequence itself is illegitimate?

They don’t negotiate with reality. They overwrite it.

They don’t change. They explain.

They don’t repair. They justify.

And the more fluent they become at avoiding consequence, the less real everyone else feels.

That’s why cruelty accelerates right after consequence gets rebranded as harm.

Because when nothing is allowed to touch you, nothing else matters.

But consequence was never punishment. It was **proof of contact**.

Proof your actions had mass. Proof other people weren’t imaginary. Proof you existed in a shared world.

They taught you to fear consequence because a person who accepts it can’t be controlled by comfort.

---

So sit with this—don’t answer fast, just think on it:

- Which consequences feel “unfair” when they show up in your relationships?
- Which costs do you believe love shouldn’t ask you to pay?

Because wherever consequence loses value, entitlement grows.

And wherever entitlement grows, power stops being human.

Not because it’s too strong—but because nothing is allowed to touch it anymore.
