> There is no such thing as lazy because that word is only thrown at you once the system has already fallen on you.

When you were producing, meeting deadlines, running yourself into the ground,

- They called you *ambitious*.
- They called you *dedicated*.
- They called you an *asset*.

The moment you slow down, the moment the weight hits, the moment your body says **stop**—they’ll call you lazy.

Not because you changed, but because the system has wrung you dry and now needs someone to blame.

Lazy is a cover story for collapse.

A label pinned to survivors of burnout, grief, fear, disconnection.

It’s how the machine hides its own failure by making yours the headline.

Because the reality is:

## Sometimes you’re tired.

- Sometimes you’re scared.
- Sometimes you’re numb.
- Sometimes you’re choosing something else.
- Sometimes you’re disconnected from greater meaning.

But you’re never lazy.

That word only exists to erase context and make you carry the guilt for being crushed.

---

So I want you to understand if they only called you lazy after:

- The bottom dropped out.
- After the support disappeared.
- After the weight hit— it’s because the word isn’t about you.

### It’s about the system that fell.

Your pause was never proof of weakness.

It was your body trying to survive in a space that stopped holding you.

---

And from there you don’t need to fix yourself.

You need to bury the lie.

You need to name what’s real.

Because once you do, the shame loses its teeth.

You stop blaming yourself for the wreckage you were left in.

## And what’s left after that is strength:

You’ll rise messy.  
You’ll rise shaking.  
But guess what you’ll do anyway…

You’ll rise.
