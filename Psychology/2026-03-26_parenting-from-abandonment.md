## Parenting from abandonment is raising a child while secretly needing them not to leave you.

Not because you're evil.

Because somewhere in your life—someone already did.

They left your body before they left the room. They made love feel conditional, temporary, fragile.

So you made a decision you never said out loud:

**"Next time… I won't be the one left."**

So when your child was born you didn't just feel love.

You felt **relief**.

Finally—someone who couldn't walk away yet.

And you held them.

But not just to raise them.

To **anchor yourself**.

You called it closeness.

But you watched them too closely. You needed them too deeply. You felt their distance before it even happened.

So you did what made sense to your body:

You shaped them.

Soft when they stayed. Heavy when they pulled away. Warm when they chose you. Wounded when they didn't.

You didn't just love your child.

**You made them responsible for your ability to survive being alone.**

You didn't say:

> "Don't leave me."

You said:

> "You've changed." "You don't care like you used to." "After everything I've done…"

**And they felt it.**

That look you gave them when they didn't come sit with you.

Not anger. Not yelling.

Just… something leaving your face.

They learned everything from that moment.

Every time they wanted space and saw your face shift

Every time they chose themselves and felt something in you… drop

Every time they realized:

**"If I become fully myself… I might lose you."**

So they made a decision too.

Not out loud.

But in their nervous system:

"I'll stay… even if I have to leave myself to do it."

They became what you needed.

Not who they were.

And this is the sentence they have never been allowed to say:

"I didn't feel like your child. I felt like the thing keeping you okay."

"I love you… but it felt like I had to shrink so you wouldn't fall apart."

If you feel the urge to explain this away—that's the exact reflex that kept it in place.

Now here's the part that usually breaks people open:

You did this because no one ever showed you how to love… without needing someone to stay.

You were surviving.

But survival is not neutral when you raise a child inside it.

So healing, for you, is not:

> **"getting them close again"**

Give up on that.

It's this:

> **learning how to feel your abandonment without turning your child into the answer**

It's letting them: pull away, disagree, become, leave the room without checking your face first

It's hearing:

**"I need space"**

and not translating it into:

**"I'm losing them"**

---

#### Stay close or I feel like I'm losing you.

---

It's standing in that ache without reaching for them to solve it.

And here's the truth you don't want:

They may never come back the way you want.

Because the version of them that stayed like that was never free.

That does happen.

Some people never leave survival.

Even when they have children.

Now.

If you're the child who grew up in this this is where it turns.

You weren't loved wrong.

You were loved **with a condition that was never spoken**

Stay close. Stay soft. Stay available. Stay mine.

---

#### Stay close or I lose them.

---

So now you walk into relationships already bending.

Already scanning.

Already asking:

> "What do I need to be so this doesn't break?"

You call it care.

But it's management.

You call it love.

But it's prevention.

You call it effort.

But it's fear.

You feel everything.

Their tone. Their distance. Their silence.

And your body says:

**"Fix it. Now."**

**It demands that of you.**

Because once upon a time you couldn't afford not to.

So let me say the sentence you've been avoiding your entire life:

You cannot heal your parent.

And you cannot earn a version of them that didn't exist when you needed it.

That door?

May not open.

And if you keep standing in front of it you will build your entire life around trying to knock correctly

---

#### Stay close or I lose this.

---

Healing for you is not:

getting them to understand

It's this:

> **learning how to leave without calling it betrayal**

> **Learning how to love without over-functioning**

> **Learning how to exist without scanning someone else's emotional weather**

Even when it feels like:

"I'm about to lose everything"

Because here's the flip:

You already lost yourself once.

That's what this cost when you're parented from abandonment

So when you enter romance be honest about what you're doing.

You think you're falling in love.

But your body is recognizing the job.

You are not just dating.

You are recreating a system.

You find people who need you **just enough**

You stay ahead of their distance **just enough**

You hold everything together **just enough**

And you call it:

"This one matters"

But it's not about them.

It's about the moment you were trained to avoid.

---

#### Stay close or this ends.

---

So here's the final thing I want you to understand.

Love is not something you stabilize.

Love is something you enter without controlling the outcome.

That's what makes it real.

They can leave.

**You can still be whole.**

Your parent may never heal.

**You still can.**

And the moment you stop organizing your life around not being abandoned—

You'll realize the door was never locked.

You were just trained to stay.
