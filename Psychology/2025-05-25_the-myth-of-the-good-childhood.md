**“Some people weren’t neglected.  
They were** ***managed.*** **Some weren’t abused.  
They were just… never** ***met.*****”**

No bruises.  
No screaming.  
No slammed doors.

Just a house where everything was fine—  
except *you were freezing, and no one noticed.*

---

“It’s hard to heal from something that doesn’t look like harm.”

You had structure.  
You had bedtime.  
You had meals and manners and birthday cakes.

But your sadness?  
Your joy?  
Your inner world?

It didn’t exist in that house.  
Not because anyone was cruel—  
but because no one was curious.

> “No one hurt you.  
> They just *never really saw you.*”

---

The body is an archive and this is what the body remembers:  
The silence after you tried to share.  
The eye roll when you got too excited.  
The quick subject change when you got too sad.”

So you stopped asking.  
You stopped shining.  
You shrunk your range until all your emotions fit inside one socially acceptable smile.

And you were praised for being easy.

> “The wound wasn’t what happened.  
> It was what *never did.*”

---

“Now you say things like:  
‘I had a good childhood. Nothing happened to me.’  
And that’s exactly the point.”

Nothing *happened*.  
No comfort.  
No mirroring.  
No one asking, *‘How does that feel?’* and meaning it.

So now you live in a loop of over-functioning,  
hoping someone finally notices the grief you weren’t allowed to feel.

> “It’s not that they didn’t love you.  
> It’s that they loved a version of you  
> that didn’t ask for anything real.”

---

“It grows in polite homes.  
Homes where the thermostat was always set—  
but the emotional climate never changed.”

No crisis.  
Just absence.  
No chaos.  
Just emptiness in a beautiful frame.

And so the child becomes the adult  
who doesn’t know how to rest unless something’s wrong.  
Who can’t feel joy unless it’s earned.  
Who mistrusts tenderness unless there’s a catch.

> “You never learned to feel safe in warmth.  
> Because warmth never stayed long enough to be trusted.”

---

“There is no villain here.  
Only a room that stayed too quiet  
for too long.”

And now that child still lives inside you—  
neat, smart, praised…  
but frozen.

You don’t need to dig for pain that never happened.  
You just need to honor the pain of what *never did*.

---

> “It’s okay if the story isn’t dramatic.  
> It’s still valid if it’s quiet.  
> You still deserve warmth.  
> You still deserve to thaw.”
