Imagine this:

You wake up and the day is already gone.

Before your feet hit the floor, it’s over.

You can’t say this out loud, because the moment you whisper “*life is meaningless,*” the stage light will turn away and the whole room will pretend they didn’t hear you.

## So You Carry It.

Like rot in your chest.

Like a scream welded shut.

You smile when you’re supposed to.

You laugh in the right places.

But every time the sound leaves your mouth, you hear the echo:

> *This isn’t me. This isn’t real.*

The void doesn’t arrive in a single night.

It leaks.

Drip by drip.

Until you’re **ankle-deep in sludge** and the only prayer you have left is silence.

And shame is the lock. Not just the emptiness, but the fact **you can’t admit it.**

Because the moment you confess you can’t find meaning, you’re marked defective.

They’ll call it weakness, laziness, failure.

They’ll leave you alone with it.  
They already have.

So the question grows teeth.

## *Does life mean anything at all?*

It gnaws your ribs.  
It crawls down your throat.

- You try to outrun it with work.
- You try to drown it with drink.
- You try to pray it into silence.

But the bones rattle louder every time.

And then—guess what comes after that.

## Collapse.

The role dies.

The story breaks.

The shadow disintegrates.

And you swear it’s the end of you.

But it isn’t.

It’s the clearing of you.

Because underneath the wreckage, there’s a seeing that never left.

The dust still hanging in the beam.

Reflecting both directions at once.

That’s what you are.

- Not the mask.
- Not the exile.
- Not the silence.

# The seeing itself.

And the grief will tear you.

You will remember the car seat that felt like a coffin.

The party where your smile cracked like glass.

The nights you almost slipped away because no one could bear to hold the truth with you.

And it will ache.

Because the dust remembers what it was like to mistake itself for shadow.

But the release is just as total.

Life doesn’t need meaning.

## Life *is* meaning.

Not as a purpose **to prove.**

Not as a story **to win.**

> But as the raw fact that seeing is happening at all.

The agony was the doorway. The void was the veil tearing. The collapse was not your failure.

## It was your unveiling.

And now you stand in the clearing.

**Ash in your mouth.**

**Blood in your breath.**

Still trembling.

But alive.

Already whole.
