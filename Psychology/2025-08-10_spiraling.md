**Spiraling is just emotional time travel without a seatbelt.**  
It looks like a meltdown, but it’s really a reunion.  
Of every version of you that got hurt **all showing up at once.**

You’re not being dramatic.  
You’re being **dragged**:

- Back to the argument no one apologized for.
- Back to the silence that turned your bedroom into a courtroom.
- Back to the moment you had to calm someone else just to survive the night.

And now?  
You’re trying to hold the wheel with five ghosts grabbing your wrists.

You don’t spiral because you’re broken.  
You spiral because **logic failed you** when you needed rescue and the only compass left is hypervigilance dressed in a bloodstained gown.

Your body is still trying to **warn the past.**  
**That past** that no one saved you from.

So every time danger feels familiar, you don’t just feel it once **you feel it in syndication.**

It’s a trauma orchestra with no conductor.  
Each instrument is a version of you that begged to be heard.  
Your chest holds reruns.  
Your nervous system plays reroutes.  
Your breath forgets which century it’s in.

You think you’re falling apart.  
But spiraling is just every abandoned self trying to finish the sentence.  
It’s not collapse.  
It’s a **reunion.**

Of every unheard “please.”  
Every swallowed scream.  
Every time you said “I’m fine” when you were bleeding inside and no one came.

The first spiral wasn’t in your twenties.  
**It was when you were nine.**And asking for help made people angry.  
When you flinched before crying.  
When fear got punished instead of held.

You became your own evacuation plan before your body even finished growing.

> And now you wonder why you fall apart alone.

Spiraling is not your failure.  
It’s your body's last-ditch attempt to gather all the versions of you that got left behind.

This isn't weakness.  
It’s your nervous system begging you to **stop leaving yourself.**

So maybe this time—don’t walk out on you.  
Even if it’s loud.  
Even if it’s messy.  
Even if it’s all happening at once.

Because spiraling isn’t the breakdown.  
It’s the **breakthrough** trying to get past your alarms.

And maybe this time… you stay.

---

You want to stop spiraling? We can look each other in the eyes about it.   
You can’t—until you name **who never showed up.**  
You can’t—until you admit what **safety cost you.**  
You can’t—until you stop trying to soothe everyone else’s comfort  
**while you bleed into the carpet.**
