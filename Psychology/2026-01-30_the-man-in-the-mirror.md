### Most men don’t face the man in the mirror.

They ask it for permission.

Animals don’t do that. They don’t linger. They don’t consult reflections. They don’t wait to be approved by glass.

Only humans stay. Only humans hover. Only humans stare like the image might grant parole.

That’s not awareness. That’s submission.

You’re not looking for truth. You’re waiting for a verdict.

Am I still useful.

Am I still viable.

Am I still worth the oxygen.

That’s not self-reflection. It’s a performance review with your own face on it.

---

The lie was courage.

They told you: “Face yourself.”

What they trained you to do was report in.

The man in the mirror isn’t you. It’s the system wearing your skin.

For women, the mirror asked: Are you desirable.

For men, the mirror asks: Are you operational.

And tools don’t get interior lives. Tools don’t get grief. Tools don’t get to stop.

They get used.

So when something in you breaks, you don’t tend to it. You recalibrate.

You call that discipline. You call that growth. You call that being a man.

---

And now the mirror is black.

It doesn’t show your face. It shows your rank. Your pace. Your relevance. How easily you can be replaced.

You don’t judge yourself anymore. You read the sentence.

Here’s the grief you never name:

You stayed at the mirror hoping it would finally look back with mercy.

It never did.

So you learned to live without mercy instead.

Stop asking the man in the mirror if you’re allowed to be the cause.

Because the moment you ask, you’ve already surrendered authority.

If you let yourself be reduced to a tool, the world will use you until you rust and discard you for one that shines.

And it will call that maturity. It will call that strength. It will call that success.

The mirror doesn’t know what your ribs are for. It doesn’t know why your chest tightens when something real tries to move.

It only knows extraction.

There was a moment—before the metrics, before the grind, before the betrayal—when the man behind your eyes stood intact.

Unmeasured. Unoptimized. Unbroken.

That wasn’t usefulness. That was presence.

The real man in the mirror was never in the usefulness it was the one time the man behind your eyes stood un-betrayed, even by himself!

That’s the wound.

Not that you failed.

But that you learned to look away from something alive to keep a dead system satisfied.

---

You were never meant to linger at the glass.

You were meant to move.

And the second you stop asking reflection to authorize you, the cage doesn’t explode—

It simply loses power.

That’s the cut.
