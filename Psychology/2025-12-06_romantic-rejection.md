People rarely reject other people.

It’s true

They usually rejecting the *fantasy* you built around them because it was the only place you ever let yourself exist.

That’s because Rejection does not hit the heart—it hits the dream where you finally believed you were real.

That’s why it feels like death.

Because it wasn’t a breakup. It was an eviction from the only home you ever built inside somebody else.

You think you’re hurting because they left.

### **You’re hurting because they took your imaginary self with them.**

The version of you that felt wanted. The version of you that felt chosen. The version of you that felt almost whole. The version of you that only existed in the light of their attention.

That “love” you miss so desperately?

**It was a dissociative universe where you finally mattered.**

Not love. Not connection. Not presence.

A **hallucination your heart crawled into to survive loneliness.**

They didn’t destroy you. They destroyed your anesthesia. Now you’re sober in a body that doesn’t know how to hold itself.

And that is why rejection feels like extinction.

Not because they were your soulmate—but because the fantasy was the closest thing you ever had to a self.

Listen.

**Everything you called love was a dissociation you mistook for destiny.**

You didn’t fall for them. You fell into the **version of you** that could only exist in their gaze.

You weren’t heartbroken. You were **de-rendered**.

You didn’t lose love. You lost the *illusion of safety* you built because the real world never taught you how to feel safe inside your own skin.

Rejection trauma is the moment the dissociative architecture collapses and the body finally screams:

**“I don’t know how to exist without someone else holding my outline.”**

That’s the real pain.

Not abandonment. Not betrayal. Not loss.

The void where your imaginary self used to live.

---

So I want you to understand:

**If a single person disappearing can cause you to disappear, you were never inside yourself to begin with.**

You were renting identity.

Leasing validation. Borrowing existence. Living on emotional credit in someone else’s imagined future.

And now the loan got called in and you don’t know what to do.

But this—this moment right here where you feel flayed open, hollow, unbearable, unrecognizable, this is really the only moment in your life where you actually have access to the truth:

**The version of you that shattered was never the version that could survive reality.**

It was a scaffolding. A placeholder. A phantom limb of identity built from longing and adrenaline and projection.

It needed to die.

And you needed to watch it die so you could finally see the part of you that was never inside their hands.

---

So I want you to know this:

**Rejection didn’t end you. It ended your dependency on being imagined by someone else.**

Everything you built in dissociation is gone.

Good.

It couldn’t hold you anyway.

Now you get to build something real: not a fantasy, not a performance, not a mirror version of yourself—

**A self that does not collapse when someone walks away.**

A self that does not evaporate in the absence of attention.

A self that does not have to beg to stay rendered.

---

#### You don’t heal rejection by being chosen again.

That’s just running the old wound in a new direction.

You heal rejection the moment you realize:

**No one else gets to decide whether you exist.**

The fantasy is dead.

Let it die.

You are the only thing in this story that was ever meant to live in reality.
