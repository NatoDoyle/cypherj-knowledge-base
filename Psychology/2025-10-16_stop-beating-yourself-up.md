> Stop beating yourself up because you keep thinking you’re the one swinging, but you’ve just been standing in the middle of every blow that was ever thrown at you.

You were never the one creating distance between you and your dreams.

You were the one absorbing it.

Every punishment that missed its target found a home in your chest.

Punishment learned how to dress like you. It borrowed **your voice, your posture, your guilt.**

It even learned how to love like you, **hesitant, apologetic, already bracing for the hit.**

You didn’t build the wall but you were born between it.

# Raised in the echo of other people’s silence.

Taught that being seen was something you had to earn, and being safe was something you had to prove.

They taught you to confuse reflection with control to think you were getting better when you were just getting smaller.

They told you pain meant you were still trying, that if it burned, it must be working.

But proof of what? What are you working for?

That you’re still willing to bleed for permission?

> That you’ll keep punishing yourself just to prove you’re not the villain in your own story?

## You let punishment get between you and living as an experience.

You started measuring your worth by how quickly you could apologize for existing.  
Every smile became a checkpoint, every silence a sentence.

And still, you tried.

You tried so hard to make peace with a voice that never once said your name with kindness.

You tried to live in the same room as yourself without flinching at the sound of your own breathing.

That counts for more than anyone ever told you.

Because that’s not failure that’s survival learning to walk upright again.

## Tomorrow isn’t coming the way you want it:

it’s moving through your awareness like weather.

A sunrise made of memory.

So who cares if dawn comes with pain?

You rose to the occasion, but came back with nothing except your eyes open to your own wounds.

And instead of saying, *“this hurt for the first time,”* you handed yourself the whip.

Because confession feels safer than confusion.

Because clarity feels like control, even when it cuts you open.

You mistake bleeding for progress, and healing for hiding the evidence.

### I Want You To Sit With Yourself.

You wonder why you don’t get anything done.

It’s because every time you’re meant to cry for people who don’t understand,  
you lie down on the altar and offer up your softness like currency.

You keep paying the world in versions of yourself.

**But the world can’t love you,** it only knows how to consume you.

That interview didn’t have a heartbeat.

That machine isn’t a worker.

That heartbreak didn’t even have a heart in it. It’s just that hollow rooms scream when they echo and you’ve mistaken the echo **for company.**

That parent did what they had to do.

Survival taught them distance as love.

## But here’s what **we’re** going to do.

- **We’re not punishing ourselves for surviving anymore.**
- **We’re not kneeling at the altar of “almost enough.”**
- **We’re going to stop calling guilt “growth.”**
- **We’re going to stop calling exhaustion “discipline.”**
- **We’re going to stop performing our pain just so someone claps for the effort.**

You don’t owe the world your suffering to prove that you care.

You just have to live close enough to feel it.

Presence is the only apology worth offering.

Awareness is the only redemption worth keeping.

## So forgive yourself, not for being wrong, but for being *far.*

You were always already here.

Every time you breathe without punishment, you close the gap another inch.

And when the world asks you to bleed again,  
say, *I’ve given enough blood to be real.*

Then walk out not healed, because it was never about that.

It was about leaving whole.
