You weren’t the bigger person.  
You were just the one who **shut up first.**And they called that strength.

You wanted to scream, but they clapped when you swallowed it.  
You wanted to name what happened, but they praised your “grace” while **editing your pain out of the story.**

You didn’t forgive.

You just got tired of **rehearsing a version of yourself** that looked healed enough to be lovable.

This is the part they won’t say out loud:

> Because being the “bigger person” wasn’t a choice—it was a **performance you learned to survive.**Because if you told the truth, it would’ve made *them* the villain.  
> And you were still trying to be the hero in their story.

You called it peace, but all I see is **emotional tax avoidance.**You learned how to flinch with a smile.  
You learned how to bleed behind your own back.  
You learned how to offer silence and call it “growth.”

They didn’t give you the high road.  
They **evicted you from your own voice** and offered you a pedestal in return.

You never got to say:

> “I don’t forgive you.”

**You never got to say,**

- “I’m still angry.”
- “You hurt me and kept moving like I was a cabinet to fit for your doorways.
- “I swallowed my tongue so you could keep your image.”
- “I lied every time I smiled.”

They don’t want the bigger person.

Here’s what they really want:  
**A** **more convenient witness.**The one who won’t press charges with their voice.  
The one who turns betrayal into a poem, abuse into a teachable moment, and erasure into elegance.

---

That’s all they want, so I want you to know.

You weren’t healed.  
You were **well-behaved.**You weren’t stronger.  
You were **just easier to digest.**

And they mistook your survival performance for peace.

So I’m answering the question tonight.

## Stop Being The Bigger Person, And Start Being The Honest One.

They told you it was power.

But when you tried it, it tasted like **silence soaked in shame.**

**They’ve turned you into a flammable liar, and now you** sit at home striking yourself with matches hoping this time it doesn’t burn you with what they gave you.

I don’t want to hear stories about how you were the bigger person.  
I want to hear when you realized they never deserved your silence.

I want to hear about the moment you woke up and said:

> “I wasn’t forgiving. I was dissociating.”  
> “I wasn’t healed. I was **hushed.**”  
> “I wasn’t mature. I was **strategically discarded.**”

---

Being the “bigger person” was never about healing.  
It was about **disqualifying yourself from clarity** so *they* didn’t have to feel shame.

They made a museum out of your restraint.  
They hung your silence on the wall and called it grace.  
But when you finally wanted to speak?

> “Too late.”  
>  “Why now?”  
>  “Let it go.”

No.  
Let **me** go.  
Let me out of the deal where I *shrink*, and you **grow**, and we call that ***balance***.

You say you took the high road?  
What did you see from up there?

**WHAT DID YOU SEE?**

Because what I see from down here, is that you left your truth behind and called it peace and I think it’s time we circle back to pick it up.
