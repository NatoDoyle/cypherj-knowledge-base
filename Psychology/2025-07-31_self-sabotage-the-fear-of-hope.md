> Self-sabotage is not the fear of failure.

It's the fear of being disappointed while fully invested.

You don't ghost your progress because you don't believe in yourself. You ghost it because last time you believed, you watched the ceiling collapse halfway through your rebuild.

And now? You don't risk foundation. **You just decorate the rubble.**

---

## "Why Do I Keep Ruining It?"

**You don't.**

You're just triggering the fire alarm before the fire can find you.

You fear hope. Your nervous system remembers:

- How bright it felt to believe.
- How hard it fell when that belief turned cold **AND** how stupid you felt standing there with effort in both hands and nothing to show for it.

So now it says:

- "Let's not get caught off guard again."
- "Let's fail on our own terms."
- "Let's end it before we hope."

That's not dysfunction. That's trauma putting its shoes on early and walking you out of your own life before it disappoints you.

# You Don't Fear Failure.

You fear the moment after you let yourself care. You fear getting good at something and watching it still leave. You fear sitting in a win that never comes.

So instead? You vanish the second it matters. You downplay every chance. You press pause so hard the remote cracks.

Because if you stop just in time—you don't have to feel what it's like to give everything and still not be chosen.

---

## "Self-sabotage" is just your body saying, "Last time we hoped, it killed us."

**You got abandoned mid-effort.**

**You got gaslit mid-dream.**

**You got laughed at mid-reach.**

Now you skip the party. Turn down the offer. Stay in bed instead of speaking life into your dream.

Because hope was once a loaded weapon pointed at your mouth, and you flinched every time you tried to speak joy into existence.

You didn't stop because you don't want it. You stopped because your body associated forward motion with betrayal.

When you reached out, they disappeared. When you showed up, they laughed. When you built something, someone kicked it over—and called it character development.

So now your nervous system is saying: "What if we just never start again?"

---

## You Want To Shake Your Etch-A-Sketch

You're going to have to stop looking at yourself like you're the antagonist in this story

You don't heal sabotage by overpowering it. You heal it by listening to the version of you that created it.

That **"Never again,"** version of you didn't just infiltrate your soul! they weren't sabotaging you. They were protecting you the only way they knew how.

It's not about letting go of that part of yourself go, it's about **finally letting it use the time machine.**

Because hope isn't a trap in the present, it's just your memory is full of liars and the call is coming from inside the house.

Your family handed your words they couldn't mean like love, dreams, hope, carelessly not even knowing you'd pack your bags with them. So unpack your bags. Because the tools you brought to protect yourself from their deception, are the ones now breeding self-hatred in your home.
